# 14b WAREWASHING AND SANITIZING PROCEDURES
OBSERVATIONS: Lack sanitizer inside dish machine in the bar. Corrected on site.

MAJOR

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food-contact surfaces and multiservice utensils shall be effectively washed, rinsed, and sanitized to remove or completely loosen soils by use of manual or mechanical methods. The temperature of the washing solution shall be maintained at least 100F or at the temperature specified by the manufacturer's label. Manual sanitation shall be accomplished during the final rinse by contact with solution containing 100 ppm available chlorine or 200 ppm quaternary ammonium. Mechanical sanitization shall be accomplished in the final rinse by achieving a utensil surface temperature of 160F or by contact with solution containing 50 ppm available chlorine. After cleaning and sanitizing, equipment and utensils shall be air dried. (114097, 114099.1, 114099.2, 114099.4, 114099.6, 114099.7, 114101 (b-d), 114105, 114109, 114111)

# 6 HANDWASHING FACILITIES
OBSERVATIONS: Found paper towels stored outside of the dispenser inside handicap stall in the women's restroom. Correct today
CODE DESCRIPTION: Handwashing facilities shall be provided within or adjacent to toilet rooms, food preparation, and warewashing areas. Handwashing facilities shall be clean, unobstructed, and accessible at all times. Handwashing facilities shall be provided with soap and sanitary single-use towels or heated-air hand drying device in approved dispensers. Dispensers shall be maintained in good repair. (113953, 113953.1, 113953.2)
# 7 HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES
OBSERVATIONS: Found two large shredded cheese containers stored at 44F inside stand up refrigerator in the pizza prep kitchen. Corrected on site.
NOTE: Door to the unit was open upon my arrival. Ambient air down to 40F.
CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return to the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food contact surfaces, utensils, and equipment shall be cleaned and sanitized at the following times: before each use with different type of raw food of animal origin, when changing from raw food to ready to eat food, between uses with raw produce and potentially hazardous food, before using a thermometer, any time during the operation when contamination may have occurred. Food-contact surfaces and utensils shall be clean to sight and touch. (114113, 114115, 114117, 114125(b), 114141) # 21 HOT AND COLD WATER SUPPLY
OBSERVATIONS: Lack hot water at hand sink in the bar. Corrected on site.

CODE DESCRIPTION: An adequate, protected, pressurized, potable supply of hot and cold water shall be provided at all times. Hot water shall be supplied at a minimum temperature of at least 120F when measured from the faucet. The temperature of water provided to handwashing facilities shall be at least 100F. Handwashing facilities equipped with auto run faucets shall have their water temperature set between 100F to 108F. # 42 GARBAGE AND REFUSE DISPOSAL
OBSERVATIONS: Found the tallow bin open outside on a day its raining. Correct immediately
Found garbage scattered throughout the dumpster enclosure. Correct immediately
CODE DESCRIPTION: The exterior premises of a food facility shall be kept free of litter and rubbish. All food waste and rubbish shall be kept in nonabsorbent, durable, cleanable, leak-proof, and rodent-proof containers. Containers shall have close-fitting lids and be covered at all times. All waste must be removed and disposed of as frequently as necessary to prevent the development of objectionable odors and other conditions that attract or harbor insects or rodents. (114244, 114245, 114245.1, 114245.2, 114245.3, 114245.4, 114245.5, 114245.6, 114245.7, 114257)
# 44c MOP SINK AND JANITORIAL AREA
OBSERVATIONS: Found the mop stored in the wet bucket. Store either in the ringer section of the mop bucket or hang in between uses to dry properly. Correct today
CODE DESCRIPTION: At least one janitorial sink equipped with hot and cold water and a drain shall be provided and conveniently located for the cleaning of mops or similar wet floor cleaning tools and for the disposal of mop water and similar liquid waste. A room, area, or cabinet separated from food preparation, food storage, or warewashing shall be provided for the storage of cleaning equipment and supplies. After use, mops shall be placed in a position that allows them to air-dry without soiling walls, equipment, or supplies. (114279, 114281, 114282)
