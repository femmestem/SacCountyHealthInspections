# 9 COOLING METHODS
OBSERVATIONS: Container of rice in the walk-in (not prepared today) with accumulated condensation on the lid, measured at 44F from the center. Outer edges measured at 42F-43F. Other containers of rice measured below 41F with no visible condensation. Corrected; item was voluntarily discarded.
CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be rapidly cooled from 135F to 70F within 2 hours, and then from 70F to 41F within 4 hours. Cooling shall be by one or more of the following methods: in shallow containers, separating food into smaller portions, adding ice as an ingredient, using an ice bath, stirring frequently, using rapid cooling equipment, or using containers that facilitate heat transfer. (114002, 114002.1)

MAJOR

# 21 HOT AND COLD WATER SUPPLY
OBSERVATIONS: One of four hand sinks in the men's restroom had water that measured at a consistent 79F. Correct within 1 day. All other hand sinks were at least 100F.
CODE DESCRIPTION: An adequate, protected, pressurized, potable supply of hot and cold water shall be provided at all times. Hot water shall be supplied at a minimum temperature of at least 120F when measured from the faucet. The temperature of water provided to handwashing facilities shall be at least 100F. Handwashing facilities equipped with auto run faucets shall have their water temperature set between 100F to 108F. (113953(c), 114101(d), 114189, 114192, 114192.1, 114195) # 33 NONFOOD-CONTACT SURFACES CLEAN
OBSERVATIONS: Floor around the grease trap under the 3-compartment sink has grease buildup. Correct today.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Nonfood-contact surfaces of equipment shall be kept free of an accumulation of dust, dirt, food residue, and other debris. Interior premises of a food facility shall be kept clean. (114115 (b), 114257)
