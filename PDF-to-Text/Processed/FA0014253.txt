# 8 TIME AS A PUBLIC HEALTH CONTROL
OBSERVATIONS: MAJOR- The following items were missing time stamps and or were labeled incorrectly: -pork buns -hot dog buns (labeled 10-2pm) -ham and cheese croissants Per owner, these items came out at 8am and was allowed to re stamp all items. A follow up inspection will be conducted within one to two weeks to verify compliance with proper use of Time as a Public Health Control (TPHC). Re inspections are billed at $268.00. Education provided at time of inspection.
CODE DESCRIPTION: When time as a public health control is used, the following procedures shall be observed: food items shall be marked to indicate four hours past the time when the food was removed from temperature control, food shall be served or discarded within the four-hour time limit, written procedures for food that was prepared, cooked, and refrigerated before time as a public health control was used, shall be maintained at the food facility and be made available to the enforcement agency upon request. (114000)
# 7 HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES
OBSERVATIONS: Measured the following potentially hazardous foods out of proper holding temperature: -prepackaged cooked ham at front display refrigerator (45F) -BBQ pork at single door reach in refrigerator (44F) Correct to a holding temperature of 41F or below immediately.
CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return to the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)
# 21 HOT AND COLD WATER SUPPLY
OBSERVATIONS: Maximum hot water achieved at ware wash sink was 113F. Provide hot water of at least 120F at the faucet. Correct within 24 hours.
CODE DESCRIPTION: An adequate, protected, pressurized, potable supply of hot and cold water shall be provided at all times. Hot water shall be supplied at a minimum temperature of at least 120F when measured from the faucet. The temperature of water provided to handwashing facilities shall be at least 100F. Handwashing facilities equipped with auto run faucets shall have their water temperature set between 100F to 108F. (113953(c), 114101(d), 114189, 114192, 114192.1, 114195)
# 23 VERMIN AND ANIMAL CONTAMINATION
OBSERVATIONS: Found 10-15 dead German cockroaches on glue traps located behind the warmers and stand up freezer (on floor). Remove all old glue traps and inform facilities pest control immediately. No live cockroaches observed during inspection; one old dead cockroach was found on floor by entrance into bakery which owner removed and discarded at time of inspection.
CODE DESCRIPTION: A food facility shall at all times be equipped, maintained, and operated as to prevent the entrance and harborage of animals, birds, and vermin, including, but not limited to rodents and insects. (114259, 114259.1, 114259.4, 114259.5)
# 35 EQUIPMENT APPROVED AND MAINTAINED
OBSERVATIONS: Non commercial microwave found in kitchen. Remove or replace with commercial equipment within 30 days.
Gaskets on 3 door reach in refrigerator are in disrepair. Repair using approved materials within 7 days.
