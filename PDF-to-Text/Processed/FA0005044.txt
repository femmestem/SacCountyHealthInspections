# 7 HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES
OBSERVATIONS: MAJOR VIOLATION - Shrimp (63F) in the left compartment of the 2-comp sink and small bowl of cooked sprouts/cabbage mix on the cook's line counter were out of proper holding temperature. Maintain all potentially hazardous foods (PHF) at proper holding temperature at all times. Shrimp was placed in the upright reach-in cooler during the inspection and veggie mix was voluntarily discarded.
MINOR VIOLATION - Cut and sliced bbq pork (49-50F) in the makeline top insert were out of proper holding temperature. Correct immediately.
CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return to the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

MAJOR

# 1a DEMONSTRATION OF KNOWLEDGE
OBSERVATIONS: Staff was not aware of proper methods of thermometer calibration and final cooking temperature of meats. Calibrate to 32F in heavily iced water and cook chcken (165F), ground pork/beef (155F), pork/beef/seafood, eggs(145F) at all times. Correct immediately.
CODE DESCRIPTION: Food employees shall have adequate knowledge of food safety as it relates to their assigned duties. (113947)
# 4 EATING, DRINKING, OR TOBACCO USE
OBSERVATIONS: Unapproved employee beverage containers observed. Cease the use of employee beverage containers with screw capped lids (e.g. water bottles, soda bottles, juice bottles, etc.) Provide beverage containers with lid and straw at all times. Correct immediately.
CODE DESCRIPTION: Employees shall not eat, drink, or use tobacco in any area where contamination of food, clean equipment and utensils, linens, and single use articles may occur. Employees may drink from closed beverage container if the container is handled to prevent contamination of the employee's hands, the container, nonprepackaged food, and food-contact surfaces. Area designated for employees to eat and drink shall be located so that food, clean equipment and utensils, linens, and single-use articles are protected from contamination. (113977, 114256 (a))
# 6 HANDWASHING FACILITIES
OBSERVATIONS: Handwash station in the server's area near the ice machine was partially covered by a pitcher. Do not place any item in or around the handwash station that may prevent handwashing. Corrected during the inspection.
Left compartment of handwash station was used to thaw shrimp but it is also used as a handwash station. Do not place any food item in this comparment that may become contaminated while washing hands. Correct immediately.
CODE DESCRIPTION: Handwashing facilities shall be provided within or adjacent to toilet rooms, food preparation, and warewashing areas. Handwashing facilities shall be clean, unobstructed, and accessible at all times. Handwashing facilities shall be provided with soap and sanitary single-use towels or heated-air hand drying device in approved dispensers. Dispensers shall be maintained in good repair. (113953, 113953.1, 113953.2)

# 26 THAWING METHODS
OBSERVATIONS: Frozen food items are being thawed too long. Maintain PHF thawing until 41F out of the refrigerator. Once the food items reaches 41F, it is ready to be prepared, cooked, or be put inside the refrigerator. If the temperature of the food item is higher than 41F, it will noted as a violation.
Shrmp was observed stored in tagnant room temperature water. Utilize one of the proper thawing procedure. Implement immediately.
CODE DESCRIPTION: Frozen foods shall be stored or displayed in their frozen state unless being thawed in the following manner: under refrigeration, completely submerged under cold running water with sufficient velocity to flush loose particles, in microwave oven, during the cooking process. (114018, 114020)
# 27 FOOD PROTECTED FROM CONTAMINATION
OBSERVATIONS: Raw chicken was stored in an unapproved bag (white, "Thank You" to-go bags) in the glass case refrigerator across the office. Utilize only food grade/clear plastic bags to directly store food items. Correct immediately.
Raw shrimp was stored above noodles in the uprigiht refrigerator. Store food items accordingly to prevent cross contamination. Correct immediately.
Two containers of tempura batter was stored at room temperature without any cover. Provide cover to al containers with food within 24 hours.
Many open bags of personal food items throughout facility. Store all open packages of food items in containers with tight fitting lids. Correct immediately.
CODE DESCRIPTION: Food shall be protected from contamination during transportation, storage, preparation, holding, and display. Food and condiments on display shall be protected from contamination by the use of dispensers, packaging, counter, service line, or sneeze guards that intercept a direct line between the consumer's mouth and the food being displayed. Only prepackaged non-potentially hazardous food or uncut produce may be displayed or sold outdoors if under overhead cover. Open-air barbecues shall be separated from public access. (113984 (a-f), 113986, 114060, 114067, 114069, 114077, 114089.1 (c), 114143 (c-e))
# 29 IDENTIFICATION AND STORAGE OF TOXIC SUBSTANCES
OBSERVATIONS: Two canisters of RAID in the office and one canister of bug spray in the private room (turned storage room) were observed. Cease the use of these unapproved pest control device within 24 hours. Discard these items within 30 days.
CODE DESCRIPTION: All poisonous substances, detergents, sanitizers, and cleaning compounds shall be stored to prevent contamination of food, equipment, utensils, linens, and single-use articles. Only pesticides that are necessary and specifically approved for use in a food facility may be used. Containers of poisonous or toxic materials shall have a legible manufacturer's label or if taken from bulk supplies shall be clearly and individually identified with the common name of the material. (114254, 114254.1, 114254.2)
# 31 CONSUMER SELF-SERVICE OPERATIONS
OBSERVATIONS: One light fixture in the glass case refrigerator across the office was not functional. Repair/replace light fixture within 7 days.

# 44c MOP SINK AND JANITORIAL AREA
OBSERVATIONS: Mop was stored in the mop bucket. Hang mop after use to allow drying. Correct immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: At least one janitorial sink equipped with hot and cold water and a drain shall be provided and conveniently located for the cleaning of mops or similar wet floor cleaning tools and for the disposal of mop water and similar liquid waste. A room, area, or cabinet separated from food preparation, food storage, or warewashing shall be provided for the storage of cleaning equipment and supplies. After use, mops shall be placed in a position that allows them to air-dry without soiling walls, equipment, or supplies. (114279, 114281, 114282)
# 44d EMPLOYEE PERSONAL ITEMS
OBSERVATIONS: Employee beverages are store above food prep counters. Store these beverage containers under or away all food and food related items within 24 hours. Correct immediately.
Food items for the staff are scattered throughout the facility. Store all employee food items in a single area under all food and food related items for he business. Corerct immediately.
CODE DESCRIPTION: No person shall store clothing or personal effects in any area used for the storage and preparation of food. Lockers or other suitable facilities shall be provided and used for the orderly storage of employee clothing and other possessions. Lockers or other suitable facilities shall be located in a designated room or area where contamination of food, equipment, utensils, and single-use articles cannot occur. (114256, 114256.1.)
# 45 FLOOR, WALL, AND CEILING FINISHES
OBSERVATIONS:
