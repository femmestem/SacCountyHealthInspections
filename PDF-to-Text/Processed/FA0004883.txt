Items listed on this report as violations do not meet the requirements set forth in the California Health and Safety Code commencing with section 7; 113700. All violations must be corrected within specified timeframe. Violations that are classified as "Major" pose an immediate threat to public health and have the potential to cause foodborne illness. # 9 COOLING METHODS
OBSERVATIONS: Teriyaki chicken at 52 F and steamed scrambled eggs at 47 F were found being improperly cooled in deep, covered containers. These were cooked "earlier this morning between 9 and 10 am". Leave containers of food items being cooled uncovered or partially open to facilitate heat transfer. Cover containers when food items being cooled reach 41 F. Corrected during the inspection.
CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be rapidly cooled from 135F to 70F within 2 hours, and then from 70F to 41F within 4 hours. Cooling shall be by one or more of the following methods: in shallow containers, separating food into smaller portions, adding ice as an ingredient, using an ice bath, stirring frequently, using rapid cooling equipment, or using containers that facilitate heat transfer. (114002, 114002.1)
# 30b FOOD CONTAINERS LABELED
OBSERVATIONS: Carafe for use of customers at the condiment station was not identified by a label (contained half & half, per operator). Correct today.
CODE DESCRIPTION: Containers holding food or food ingredients that are removed from their original packaging shall be identified with the common name of the food. (114051)
# 33 NONFOOD-CONTACT SURFACES CLEAN
OBSERVATIONS: Heavy dust accumulation observed on ceiling vents/ducts (pointed out to operator). Correct within 3 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Nonfood-contact surfaces of equipment shall be kept free of an accumulation of dust, dirt, food residue, and other debris. Interior premises of a food facility shall be kept clean. (114115 (b), 114257)
# 36 EQUIPMENT STORED IN APPROVED LOCATION
OBSERVATIONS: Container for kitchen tools and utensils (including whisks and ladles was unclean. Wash and sanitize containers and contents today. Clean regularly.
Panini maker spatula was observed not controlled by either temperature or time. Operator stated that this stays at the panini staition all day. Operator put the panini in a container with ice/water in it during the inspection.
CODE DESCRIPTION: Clean equipment, utensils, linens, and single-use articles shall be stored at least six inches above the floor in an approved location within the fully enclosed permitted food facility. Clean equipment, utensils, linens, and single-use articles shall not be exposed to splash, dust, vermin or other forms of contamination. Non-food items shall be stored and displayed separate from food and food-contact surfaces. During pauses in food preparation utensils shall be stored with handles above the top of the food, on a clean sanitized surface, in running water, or in water that is at least 135F. Single-use articles and multiservice utensils shall be handled, displayed, and dispensed so that contamination of food and lip-contact surfaces is prevented. Pressurized cylinders shall be securely fastened to rigid structure. (114074, 114081, 114119, 114121, 114161, 114172, 114178, 114179, 114083, 114185, 114185.2, 114185.3, 114185.4, 114185.5)
# 45 FLOOR, WALL, AND CEILING FINISHES
OBSERVATIONS: One large tile missing from floor with some slime buildup observed in front of the Chinese salad station. Clean area today and replace tile within 15 days.
