Items listed on this report as violations do not meet the requirements set forth in the California Health and Safety Code commencing with section 7; 113700. # 9 COOLING METHODS

OBSERVATIONS: Brown rice in 12 quart container held in the upright refrigerator, measured at 60 degrees f. Per discussion with staff rice was placed in the refrigerator yesterday. Improper cooling. Voluntarily discarded 10 quarts of brown rice. All other rice containers in the refrigerator measured below 41 degrees f. Provided instruction for proper cooling during inspection, also provided written procedure for proper cooling. Corrected.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be rapidly cooled from 135F to 70F within 2 hours, and then from 70F to 41F within

4 hours. Cooling shall be by one or more of the following methods: in shallow containers, separating food into smaller portions, adding ice as an ingredient, using an ice bath, stirring frequently, using rapid cooling equipment, or using containers that facilitate heat transfer.

(114002, 114002.1)

# 1a DEMONSTRATION OF KNOWLEDGE

OBSERVATIONS: Staff unaware of how to measure food temperature and how to calibrate thermometer. Provided instruction and demonstration during inspection.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food employees shall have adequate knowledge of food safety as it relates to their assigned duties. (113947)

# 7 HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES
OBSERVATIONS: Stream rice in crock pot measured at 130 degrees f. Improper hot holding. Maintain at 135 degrees f and above for hot holding. Staff reheated the rice to 165 degrees f during this inspection then returned the rice to the crockpot for hot holding. Note: per discussion with staff, if rice is too hot it will cook the raw fish that is served as a menu item. Discussed with staff the option of using Time as A Public Health Control for the brown rice and white rice. Provided educational pamphlets and instruction. Operator chooses to use TPHC for brown rice and white rice from this day forward.
CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return to the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)
# 27 FOOD PROTECTED FROM CONTAMINATION
OBSERVATIONS: Cooked frozen shrimp stored below the raw frozen tuna in the freezer. Improper food storage. Store cooked, and ready to eat food items on top shelf, above raw fish product. Correct within 2 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food shall be protected from contamination during transportation, storage, preparation, holding, and display. Food and condiments on display shall be protected from contamination by the use of dispensers, packaging, counter, service line, or sneeze guards that intercept a direct line between the consumer's mouth and the food being displayed. Only prepackaged non-potentially hazardous food or uncut produce may be displayed or sold outdoors if under overhead cover. Open-air barbecues shall be separated from public access. (113984 (a-f), 113986, 114060, 114067, 114069, 114077, 114089.1 (c), 114143 (c-e))
