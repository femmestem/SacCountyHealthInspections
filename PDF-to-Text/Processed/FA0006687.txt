# 7 HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES
OBSERVATIONS: The following items were observed out of temperature:

-flat of shelled eggs at 56F -Canadian bacon at 56F

During start of inspection eggs were on top of hamburger freezer. There was no visible time stamp on them. Employee relocated them to back prep table near walk-in refrigerator. They were then relocated into walk-in refrigerator. Manager stated that during the breakfast service they usually have the flat of eggs out on the counter with a discard time of 30 minutes after it is removed from temperature control. It was unclear why they were relocated into walk-in refrigerator. Manager stated they must throw them out after 30 minutes. Any time eggs are out on counter ensure the discard time is clearly labeled on each flat of eggs. It can be written directly on the carton. When the time is up in 30 minutes the remaining eggs must be discarded. They can not be placed back into refrigeration for later use. Corrected by voluntarily discarding eggs.

# 23 VERMIN AND ANIMAL CONTAMINATION
OBSERVATIONS: Approximately 10 flies were observed in dining area especially near door closest to soda machine and pole by front registers. Provide an operational fly light in dining area. Remove flies within 24 hours.
CODE DESCRIPTION: A food facility shall at all times be equipped, maintained, and operated as to prevent the entrance and harborage of animals, birds, and vermin, including, but not limited to rodents and insects. (114259, 114259.1, 114259.4, 114259.5)
# 33 NONFOOD-CONTACT SURFACES CLEAN
OBSERVATIONS: -Grease accumulation on floor under fryer and around wheels. Clean and maintain within 7 days.
-Slime like accumulation and debris inside floor sink near CO2 tanks, soda syrup boxes, and drive through window. Clean and maintain within 7 days.
-Debris on floor around CO2 tanks and around ice machine. Clean and maintain within 7 days.
CODE DESCRIPTION: Nonfood-contact surfaces of equipment shall be kept free of an accumulation of dust, dirt, food residue, and other debris. Interior premises of a food facility shall be kept clean. (114115 (b), 114257)
# 35 EQUIPMENT APPROVED AND MAINTAINED
OBSERVATIONS: -Deteriorated gasket seal inside reach-in refrigerator along prep line. Replace gasket within 30 days.
-Floor care dispenser is leaking water that is splashing into mop sink and floor. Service call was placed for this with the response time of Monday. Remove all standing water throughout the day until repair can be made.
CODE DESCRIPTION: Utensils and equipment shall be kept fully operative and in good repair. All new and replacement equipment shall be properly installed and be certified by NSF, UL Sanitation, or ETL Sanitation. Facilities that wash, rinse, soak, thaw, or similarly prepare foods shall be provided with a approved food preparation sink. Surfaces such as cutting blocks and boards that are subject to scratching and scoring shall be resurfaced or replaced if they can no longer be effectively cleaned and sanitized. (114130, 114130.1, 114130.2, 114130.3, 114130.4, 114130.5, 114130.6, 114132, 114133, 114137, 114139, 114153, 114163, 114165, 114169, 114175, 114177, 114180)
# 41 PLUMBING FIXTURES AND BACKFLOW PREVENTION
OBSERVATIONS: Drainage line from CO2/water filter box is not directly over the floor sink allowing water to flow onto the floor. Reposition pipe so that is drains into floor sink. Ensure that the end of the pipe and top rim of sink has a gap of a minimum of one inch. Correct within 7 days.
CODE DESCRIPTION: Steam tables, ice machines, ice bins, food preparation sinks, display cases, walk-in refrigeration units, and other similar equipment that discharge liquid waste shall drain into a floor sink with an air gap or other approved type of receptor. Plumbing and plumbing fixtures shall be maintained so as to prevent contamination, shall be kept clean, fully operative, and in good repair. The potable water supply shall be protected with a backflow or back siphonage protection device. Any hose used for potable water shall be of approved materials, labeled, properly stored, and used for no other purpose. (114171, 114190, 114192, 114193, 114193.1, 114199, 114201, 114269)
# 44c MOP SINK AND JANITORIAL AREA
OBSERVATIONS: Mop sink is clogged with dirty standing water. No other floor sinks or drains are effected. Correct within 24 hours.
