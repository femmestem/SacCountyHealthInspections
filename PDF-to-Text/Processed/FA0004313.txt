# 14a FOOD CONTACT SURFACES CLEANED AND SANITIZED
OBSERVATIONS: Mold accumulation on ceiling inside ice machine. Empty -> wash -> rinse -> sanitize -> air dry within 5 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food contact surfaces, utensils, and equipment shall be cleaned and sanitized at the following times: before each use with different type of raw food of animal origin, when changing from raw food to ready to eat food, between uses with raw produce and potentially hazardous food, before using a thermometer, any time during the operation when contamination may have occurred. Food-contact surfaces and utensils shall be clean to sight and touch. (114113, 114115, 114117, 114125(b), 114141) # 33 NONFOOD-CONTACT SURFACES CLEAN
OBSERVATIONS: Heave grease accumulation on ceiling especially around hood in kitchen and on walls. Remove and clean all within 14 days.
Unclean shelving observed before walk-in and above and below microwave. Clean all within 3 days.
Severe rust accumulation observed inside ice machine. If accumulation cannot be removed, replace microwave with commercial grade equipment within 60 days.
Thick black dust accumulation on lights in kitchen. Clean within 7 days.
Grime accumulation on dish racks making dish racks no longer cleanable or sanitary for use. Replace racks within 14 days.
Grease accumulation on acoustic ceiling between kitchen and front counter. If ceiling cannot be cleaned, acoustic ceiling must be removed and ceiling must be made to be smooth, easily cleanable and non-absorbent within 3 months.
CODE DESCRIPTION: Nonfood-contact surfaces of equipment shall be kept free of an accumulation of dust, dirt, food residue, and other debris. Interior premises of a food facility shall be kept clean. (114115 (b), 114257) # 38 VENTILATION AND LIGHTING
OBSERVATIONS: Existing hood and pizza oven are dis-proportional with hood not over hanging pizza oven at all. Heavy grease accumulation observed on ceiling and walls due to inadequate size of hood. If grease accumulation persists, EMD management will be consulted as to required changes that will need to be made. Ensure hood equipment is routinely cleaned and surfaces in kitchen are maintained grease free.
Gaps observed in between hood filters in hood. Eliminate gaps to ensure grease is filtered. Correct within 7 days.
CODE DESCRIPTION: Exhaust hoods shall be provided to remove toxic gases, heat, grease, vapors and smoke and be approved by health and building department. Canopy-type hoods shall extend six inches beyond all cooking equipment. All areas shall have sufficient ventilation to facilitate proper food storage. Toilet rooms shall be vented to the outside air by a screened openable window, air shaft, or light-switch activated exhaust fan. Adequate lighting shall be provided in all areas to facilitate cleaning and inspection. Light fixtures in areas where open food is stored, served, prepared, and where utensils are washed shall be of shatterproof construction or protected with light shields. (114149, 114149.1, 114149.2, 114149.3, 114252, 114252.1) # 39 TEMPERATURE MEASURING DEVICES
OBSERVATIONS: Probe thermometer uncalibrated. Provided instruction.

# 40 WIPING CLOTHS STORAGE AND USE
OBSERVATIONS: Unclean wiping cloth on counter observed with 0ppm chlorine. Corrected.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Wiping cloths used to clean food and nonfood-contact surfaces shall be used only once unless kept in clean water with a sanitizer solution of 100 ppm available chlorine or 200 ppm quaternary ammonium. Sponges shall not be used on food-contact surfaces. Except for fabric implements, linen shall not be used in contact with food. (114099.6, 114135, 114185.1, 114185.3 (d-e)).
# 41 PLUMBING FIXTURES AND BACKFLOW PREVENTION
OBSERVATIONS: Pipes draining into floorsink below dough counter lack 1-inch air gap. Provide a minimum of 1-inch gap between pipe and highest part of floorsink. Correct within 7 days.
CODE DESCRIPTION: Steam tables, ice machines, ice bins, food preparation sinks, display cases, walk-in refrigeration units, and other similar equipment that discharge liquid waste shall drain into a floor sink with an air gap or other approved type of receptor. Plumbing and plumbing fixtures shall be maintained so as to prevent contamination, shall be kept clean, fully operative, and in good repair. The potable water supply shall be protected with a backflow or back siphonage protection device. Any hose used for potable water shall be of approved materials, labeled, properly stored, and used for no other purpose. (114171, 114190, 114192, 114193, 114193.1, 114199, 114201, 114269)
# 44d EMPLOYEE PERSONAL ITEMS
OBSERVATIONS: Employee water bottle on counter next to microwave. Store all personal belongings below/away from food or related equipment. Personal beverages must be in closed containers with a lid and a straw. Water bottles are not approved.
CODE DESCRIPTION: No person shall store clothing or personal effects in any area used for the storage and preparation of food. Lockers or other suitable facilities shall be provided and used for the orderly storage of employee clothing and other possessions. Lockers or other suitable facilities shall be located in a designated room or area where contamination of food, equipment, utensils, and single-use articles cannot occur. (114256, 114256.1.)
# 44e VERMIN AND ANIMAL EXCLUSION
OBSERVATIONS: Hole in wall by floor below ADT unit in wall at front counter exceed 1/4 inch. Seal leaving surface smooth, easily cleanable and non-absorbent to prevent entry of vermin within 30 days.
CODE DESCRIPTION: A food facility shall at all times be constructed, equipped, and maintained as to prevent the entrance and harborage of animals, birds, and vermin, including, but not limited to, rodents and insects. Doors opening to outside air shall have a self-closing device and have no gaps greater than 1/4 inch when closed. Insect control devices shall be installed so that the devices are not located over a food or utensil handling areas. Pass-thru windows shall be of an approved size and be equipped with a self-closing device and air curtain if required. (114259, 114259.2, 114259.3)
