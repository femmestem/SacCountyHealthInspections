# 7 HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES
OBSERVATIONS: The following items were observed out of temperature:
-Rice on small cart by wok line was at 113F. - Fried chicken at 97F on small table across from prep sink. -Fried eggrolls at 100F on small table across from prep sink. -raw pork at 70F in large pot on drainboard of prep sink. No active prep was observed at the time of inspection.
Per manager these items were only out for an hour. All potentially hazardous foods must be kept either cold at/below 41F or hot at/above 135F.
All food was relocated into working refrigeration.
Prep refrigerator:
-cooked beef at 52F. cooked chicken at 66F. Pooled eggs at 58F. Refrigeration is working properly but these items were inside bowls which were then placed inside inserts. The inserts were double panned. Discontinue practice and place food directly inside insert and remove bowl.
CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return to the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

# 4 EATING, DRINKING, OR TOBACCO USE
OBSERVATIONS: Employee coffee cup stored on shelving above prep refrigerator. Keep employee beverages below and away from food prep surfaces, areas and storage. Drink was relocated to bottom of empty cart at end of wok line.
CODE DESCRIPTION: Employees shall not eat, drink, or use tobacco in any area where contamination of food, clean equipment and utensils, linens, and single use articles may occur. Employees may drink from closed beverage container if the container is handled to prevent contamination of the employee's hands, the container, nonprepackaged food, and food-contact surfaces. Area designated for employees to eat and drink shall be located so that food, clean equipment and utensils, linens, and single-use articles are protected from contamination. (113977, 114256 (a))
# 14a FOOD CONTACT SURFACES CLEANED AND SANITIZED
OBSERVATIONS: Buildup observed on piercing part of can opener. Clean and sanitize today.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food contact surfaces, utensils, and equipment shall be cleaned and sanitized at the following times: before each use with different type of raw food of animal origin, when changing from raw food to ready to eat food, between uses with raw produce and potentially hazardous food, before using a thermometer, any time during the operation when contamination may have occurred. Food-contact surfaces and utensils shall be clean to sight and touch. (114113, 114115, 114117, 114125(b), 114141)

# 23 VERMIN AND ANIMAL CONTAMINATION
OBSERVATIONS: Two dead American cockroaches were observed under cook's line in dishwashing area. One dead American cockroach was observed in dry storage area. Facility has Pinnacle Pest Control and they provided service on 9/9/15. During inspection Pinnacle came on site for follow up per facility's request. Follow all recommendations from pest control company and make the repairs. Continue with pest control.
CODE DESCRIPTION: A food facility shall at all times be equipped, maintained, and operated as to prevent the entrance and harborage of animals, birds, and vermin, including, but not limited to rodents and insects. (114259, 114259.1, 114259.4, 114259.5)
# 33 NONFOOD-CONTACT SURFACES CLEAN
OBSERVATIONS: -Food accumulation on bottom of 3 door stand up freezer. Clean and maintain within 3 days.
-Food/grease accumulation in between the fryers and the sides of cooking equipment. Food/grease accumulation on end of cook's line where three compartment sink is located. Wok carts have grease and food accumulation on them. Clean and maintain free of grease and food accumulation. Correct within 7 days.
-Food accumulation on handles of prep sink. Ensure sinks are clean and sanitized to prevent contamination. Correct within 24 hours.
CODE DESCRIPTION: Nonfood-contact surfaces of equipment shall be kept free of an accumulation of dust, dirt, food residue, and other debris. Interior premises of a food facility shall be kept clean. (114115 (b), 114257)
# 35 EQUIPMENT APPROVED AND MAINTAINED
OBSERVATIONS: -Magic Chef microwave is household. Provide commercial/ANSI approved equipment within 4 months.
-Cardboard used to line the shelving inside dry storage room and shelf in dishwashing area. Remove cardboard. Surfaces must be smooth, easily cleanable and non-absorbant. Correct within 3 days.
-Wood blocks used to prop up BBQ oven. Remove blocks and provide stainless steel legs with a six inch clearance. Correct within 30 days.
CODE DESCRIPTION: Utensils and equipment shall be kept fully operative and in good repair. All new and replacement equipment shall be properly installed and be certified by NSF, UL Sanitation, or ETL Sanitation. Facilities that wash, rinse, soak, thaw, or similarly prepare foods shall be provided with a approved food preparation sink. Surfaces such as cutting blocks and boards that are subject to scratching and scoring shall be resurfaced or replaced if they can no longer be effectively cleaned and sanitized. (114130, 114130.1, 114130.2, 114130.3, 114130.4, 114130.5, 114130.6, 114132, 114133, 114137, 114139, 114153, 114163, 114165, 114169, 114175, 114177, 114180)
# 36 EQUIPMENT STORED IN APPROVED LOCATION
OBSERVATIONS: Woks stored on floor under cook's line. Store on a clean and sanitized shelf or table. Correct within 2 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Clean equipment, utensils, linens, and single-use articles shall be stored at least six inches above the floor in an approved location within the fully enclosed permitted food facility. # 41 PLUMBING FIXTURES AND BACKFLOW PREVENTION
OBSERVATIONS: Provide a screen over the drain under the fryers in area with prep refrigerator. Correct within 7 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Steam tables, ice machines, ice bins, food preparation sinks, display cases, walk-in refrigeration units, and other similar equipment that discharge liquid waste shall drain into a floor sink with an air gap or other approved type of receptor. Plumbing and plumbing fixtures shall be maintained so as to prevent contamination, shall be kept clean, fully operative, and in good repair. The potable water supply shall be protected with a backflow or back siphonage protection device. Any hose used for potable water shall be of approved materials, labeled, properly stored, and used for no other purpose. (114171, 114190, 114192, 114193, 114193.1, 114199, 114201, 114269)
# 44a PREMISES KEPT FREE OF UNUSED EQUIPMENT
OBSERVATIONS: Small prep refrigerator on cook's line in dishwashing area is not in use. Remove from facility or place in operation. Correct within 14 days.
CODE DESCRIPTION: The premises of a food facility shall be kept free of items that are unnecessary to the operation or maintenance of the facility, such as equipment that is nonfunctional or no longer used. (114257.1.)
# 44c MOP SINK AND JANITORIAL AREA
OBSERVATIONS: Mops stored face down in mop buckets and sink. Hang mops on rack to allow them properly air dry. Correct within 2 days.
CODE DESCRIPTION: At least one janitorial sink equipped with hot and cold water and a drain shall be provided and conveniently located for the cleaning of mops or similar wet floor cleaning tools and for the disposal of mop water and similar liquid waste. A room, area, or cabinet separated from food preparation, food storage, or warewashing shall be provided for the storage of cleaning equipment and supplies. After use, mops shall be placed in a position that allows them to air-dry without soiling walls, equipment, or supplies. (114279, 114281, 114282)
# 45 FLOOR, WALL, AND CEILING FINISHES
OBSERVATIONS: -Gaps and cracks in between wood shelving inside dry storage room. Use sealant to seal all cracks and crevices.
-Gap observed in cove base near side entrance where prep sink is located.
-Walls and floor under prep sink are unclean. Clean and maintain within 7 days.
CODE DESCRIPTION: Food facilities shall be fully enclosed in a building consisting of permanent floors, walls, and an overhead structure that meets minimum standards. The walls and ceiling shall be smooth, durable, nonabsorbent, and easily cleanable. Flooring and base coving shall be smooth, durable, and made of approved nonabsorbent material that is easily cleanable. Floor surfaces shall be coved at the juncture of the floor and wall with a 3/8 inch minimum radius and shall extend up the wall at least 4 inches. (114143 (d), 114266, 114268, 114268.1, 114271, 114272)
