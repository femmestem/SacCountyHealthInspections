# 7 HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES

OBSERVATIONS: Spanish rice/ henny penny/107 degrees f; beef tongue/steam table/127 degrees f; carne asada/steam table/125 degrees f; pastor/steam table/126 degrees f; spanish rice/steam table/127 degrees f; All food items held for 1 hour and still within hot holding time frame. Instructed staff to reheat all food items to 165 degrees f, and return to steam table and henny penny for hot holding at 135 degrees f and above. Steam table hot water measured at 132 degrees f. Corrected during inspection. note: per discussion with staff, steam table and henny penny are turned on at the same time food is placed for hot holding. Instructed staff to turn on steam table and henny penny ahead of time to heat up to 160 degrees f prior to hot holding food.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed

containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return

to the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

# 6 HANDWASHING FACILITIES

OBSERVATIONS: Hand wash sink in the food prep area partially blocked by bucket of oil. Keep area in front of hand sink clear at all times. Corrected.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Handwashing facilities shall be provided within or adjacent to toilet rooms, food preparation, and warewashing areas. Handwashing facilities shall be clean, unobstructed, and accessible at all times. Handwashing facilities shall be provided with soap and sanitary single-use towels or heated-air hand drying device in approved dispensers. Dispensers shall be maintained in good repair. (113953, 113953.1, 113953.2)
# 9 COOLING METHODS
OBSERVATIONS: Observed that spanish rice is placed in plastic bags for cooling. Depth of food is greater than 2 inches. Not ideal for rapid cooling. Instructed staff to place food items in shallow trays for cooling. Food item did not exceed critical control point. Per staff, rice cooling in bag for approximately 1 hour @ 80 degrees f. Proivided proper cooling instructional hand out. Timed rice for 1 hour, reached 71 degrees f at 2 hours. Rice placed into walk in refrigerator at 2 hour mark.
CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be rapidly cooled from 135F to 70F within 2 hours, and then from 70F to 41F within 4 hours. Cooling shall be by one or more of the following methods: in shallow containers, separating food into smaller portions, adding ice as an ingredient, using an ice bath, stirring frequently, using rapid cooling equipment, or using containers that facilitate heat transfer. (114002, 114002.1)
# 14b WAREWASHING AND SANITIZING PROCEDURES
OBSERVATIONS: Observed staff wash, rinse, then proceed to walk away with the tray. Intervened and instructed employee to sanitize. Employee said he was not going to use the tray. Instructed staff to wash, rinse, sanitize, air day all equipment at all times. Corrected during inspection.

Upon arrival, sanitizing compartment was filled with water but did not have sanitizer. Discussed with operator to put sanitizer. (no active dish washing occurring) She stated that sanitizer is added at time dishes will be washed. Forty five minutes later observed employee wash, rinse and did not sanitize a tray. Intervened. Employee added sanitizer to the compartment and sanitized tray. Instructed operator to have sanitizing compartment available with sanitizer and sanitizig bucket set up and available prior to initiating at the beginning of the shift and prior to food preparation.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food-contact surfaces and multiservice utensils shall be effectively washed, rinsed, and sanitized to remove or completely loosen soils by use of manual or mechanical methods. The temperature of the washing solution shall be maintained at least 100F or at the temperature specified by the manufacturer's label. Manual sanitation shall be accomplished during the final rinse by contact with solution containing 100 ppm available chlorine or 200 ppm quaternary ammonium. # 32a FOOD PRESENTATION AND LABELING
OBSERVATIONS: The following food items do not have a label: 1) Carne Seca (beef jerky) 2) Chicarones in lidded containers 3) Crema held in the produce refrigerator 4) Various salsas held in the produce refrigerator Provide labels that include the business name, address, ingredient (if 2 or more), quantity of food. Correct within 2 days.
CODE DESCRIPTION: Food offered for human consumption shall be honestly presented in a way that does not mislead or misinform the consumer. Food packaged in a food facility shall bear a label that complies with the labeling requirements prescribed by the Sherman Food, Drug, and Cosmetic Law which include: common name of the food, ingredients list, quantity of contents, name and address of manufacturer, nutritional information as specified in 21 C.F.R. (114087, 114088, 114089, 114089.1(a, b), 114090, 114093.1)
# 32b COMPLIANCE WITH TRANS FAT BAN
OBSERVATIONS: Pastries in pastry cabinet, label says contains partially hydrogenated soybean oil. No trans fat information available. 2nd repeat violation. Cease selling pastries. Provide trans fat information. Operator may resume selling pastries when inspector receives trans fat information. Trans fat not to exceed 0.5grams. Provide proof of correction to inspector within 7 days. email or fax email address: Oetzelm@saccounty.net fax: 916-875-8513
CODE DESCRIPTION: No food containing artificial trans fat may be stored, distributed, served by, or used in the preparation of any food within a food facility. Every food facility shall maintain on the premises, the label for any food that contains fat, oil, or shortening, for as long as this food is stored, distributed, served by, or used in the preparation of food within the food facility. Food sold or served in a manufacturer's original sealed package is exempt from this requirement. (114377)

# 44c MOP SINK AND JANITORIAL AREA
OBSERVATIONS: Washing machine situated on top of mop sink. Unable to gain access to mop sink for janitorial purposes. Remove washing machine. Correct within 7 days. Note: Water heater not sized to include the water usage for the washing machine. Plan check not contacted by operator.
CODE DESCRIPTION: At least one janitorial sink equipped with hot and cold water and a drain shall be provided and conveniently located for the cleaning of mops or similar wet floor cleaning tools and for the disposal of mop water and similar liquid waste. A room, area, or cabinet separated from food preparation, food storage, or warewashing shall be provided for the storage of cleaning equipment and supplies. After use, mops shall be placed in a position that allows them to air-dry without soiling walls, equipment, or supplies. (114279, 114281, 114282)
# 44e VERMIN AND ANIMAL EXCLUSION
OBSERVATIONS: Upon arrival, back door open. Observed 4 flies in the facility. . Door closed during inspection. Provide screen door. Correct within 14 days.
Insect control device mounted on wall above the meat slicer. Remove insect control device from food preparation area, and move to an area where there is no food preparation, no food processing, no food and equipment storage. Correct within 2 days.
CODE DESCRIPTION: A food facility shall at all times be constructed, equipped, and maintained as to prevent the entrance and harborage of animals, birds, and vermin, including, but not limited to, rodents and insects. Doors opening to outside air shall have a self-closing device and have no gaps greater than 1/4 inch when closed. Insect control devices shall be installed so that the devices are not located over a food or utensil handling areas. Pass-thru windows shall be of an approved size and be equipped with a self-closing device and air curtain if required. (114259, 114259.2, 114259.3)
