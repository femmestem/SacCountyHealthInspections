# 1b FOOD SAFETY CERTIFICATION
OBSERVATIONS: Food safety certificate is not available. On the last inspection, Owner registered for a class at time of inspection, however, Owner failed to take the exam. Per Owner, a staff does have a food safety certificate. Acquire within 7 days and maintain on-site. CHRONIC REPEAT VIOLATION
NOTE: A reinspection will be done within 7 days. If Owner fails to provide a food safety certificate at this point, a mandatory compliance conference will be required at the Owner's cost.
CODE DESCRIPTION: Food facilities that prepare, handle, or serve non-prepackaged potentially hazardous food, shall have an employee who has passed an approved food safety certification examination. (113947.1)
# 5a HANDWASHING PROCEDURES
OBSERVATIONS: Observed cook return from dining area and then continue to handle a knife and chop meat without washing hands. Staff hands never came into contact with meat products, however, staff did touch the knife. Provided education on proper handwashing. Staff sent to wash hands and knife cleaned/sanitized.
CODE DESCRIPTION: Employees are required to wash their hands in the following situations: before engaging in food preparation, after touching bare skin, after using the toilet room, after coughing or sneezing, after eating, drinking, or using tobacco, after handling soiled equipment or utensils, as often as necessary during food preparation to prevent cross-contamination, when switching from working with raw to ready to eat foods, before donning gloves, before dispensing or serving food, after engaging in other activities that may contaminate hands. (113952, 113953.3, 113953.4)
# 6 HANDWASHING FACILITIES
OBSERVATIONS: Main hand sink in kitchen partially blocked. Keep fully accessible. Corrected.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Handwashing facilities shall be provided within or adjacent to toilet rooms, food preparation, and warewashing areas. Handwashing facilities shall be clean, unobstructed, and accessible at all times. Handwashing facilities shall be provided with soap and sanitary single-use towels or heated-air hand drying device in approved dispensers. Dispensers shall be maintained in good repair. (113953, 113953.1, 113953.2)
# 9 COOLING METHODS
OBSERVATIONS: Cooked noodles located on self serve service line measured from 53-60F. Remaining food temperatures measured at/below 41F. Per staff, items were prepared 30 minutes prior to inspection and was relocated directly into service line. Discontinue practice. Cool rapidly to 41F and below and then place onto self serve units. These particular units are not designed to cool potentially hazardous foods, but, rather hold temperatures. Items removed into freezers for a quick chill. Provided education. 2ND REPEAT VIOLATION
Bus tubs of cooked noodles in walk in cooler with a tight fitting lid measured from 53-66F. Items made 30 minutes prior to inspection. Do not cover until at/below 41F to allow rapid cooling. Corrected.
CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be rapidly cooled from 135F to 70F within 2 hours, and then from 70F to 41F within 4 hours. Cooling shall be by one or more of the following methods: in shallow containers, separating food into smaller portions, adding ice as an ingredient, using an ice bath, stirring frequently, using rapid cooling equipment, or using containers that facilitate heat transfer. (114002, 114002.1)

# 14a FOOD CONTACT SURFACES CLEANED AND SANITIZED
OBSERVATIONS: Vegetable shredder and meat slicer observed with old food/grease/residue like accumulation. Correct within 24 hours.
CODE DESCRIPTION: Food contact surfaces, utensils, and equipment shall be cleaned and sanitized at the following times: before each use with different type of raw food of animal origin, when changing from raw food to ready to eat food, between uses with raw produce and potentially hazardous food, before using a thermometer, any time during the operation when contamination may have occurred. Food-contact surfaces and utensils shall be clean to sight and touch. (114113, 114115, 114117, 114125(b), 114141)
# 26 THAWING METHODS
OBSERVATIONS: Found several boxes of frozen meat left unattended throughout kitchen to thaw. Practice proper thawing methods. Items relocated into a cooler.
CODE DESCRIPTION: Frozen foods shall be stored or displayed in their frozen state unless being thawed in the following manner: under refrigeration, completely submerged under cold running water with sufficient velocity to flush loose particles, in microwave oven, during the cooking process. (114018, 114020)

# 35 EQUIPMENT APPROVED AND MAINTAINED
OBSERVATIONS: Rust observed on walk in cooler wall and several food storage racks. Repair/replace within 30 days. 2ND REPEAT VIOLATION.
1 of 2 handles on double handled knife is damaged. Replace knife within 7 days.
Cardboard used to line several shelves in kitchen. Discontinue use. Utilize a material that is smooth, durable, easily cleanable, and non absorbent. Correct within 24 hours.
Lids on several bulk food containers in dry storage are damaged. Replace within 30 days.
CODE DESCRIPTION: Utensils and equipment shall be kept fully operative and in good repair. All new and replacement equipment shall be properly installed and be certified by NSF, UL Sanitation, or ETL Sanitation. Facilities that wash, rinse, soak, thaw, or similarly prepare foods shall be provided with a approved food preparation sink. Surfaces such as cutting blocks and boards that are subject to scratching and scoring shall be resurfaced or replaced if they can no longer be effectively cleaned and sanitized. (114130, 114130.1, 114130.2, 114130.3, 114130.4, 114130.5, 114130.6, 114132, 114133, 114137, 114139, 114153, 114163, 114165, 114169, 114175, 114177, 114180) # 38 VENTILATION AND LIGHTING
OBSERVATIONS: Observed 1 non functional light bulb under kitchen hood ventilation. Correct within 7 days. 3RD REPEAT VIOLATION.
CODE DESCRIPTION: Exhaust hoods shall be provided to remove toxic gases, heat, grease, vapors and smoke and be approved by health and building department. Canopy-type hoods shall extend six inches beyond all cooking equipment. All areas shall have sufficient ventilation to facilitate proper food storage. Toilet rooms shall be vented to the outside air by a screened openable window, air shaft, or light-switch activated exhaust fan. Adequate lighting shall be provided in all areas to facilitate cleaning and inspection. Light fixtures in areas where open food is stored, served, prepared, and where utensils are washed shall be of shatterproof construction or protected with light shields. (114149, 114149.1, 114149.2, 114149.3, 114252, 114252.1) # 40 WIPING CLOTHS STORAGE AND USE
OBSERVATIONS: Soiled wipe cloths on counter measured at 0ppm chlorine. No buckets were observed either in kitchen or Mongolian bbq griddle line. Provided education. Correct now. 2ND REPEAT VIOLATION
CODE DESCRIPTION: Wiping cloths used to clean food and nonfood-contact surfaces shall be used only once unless kept in clean water with a sanitizer solution of 100 ppm available chlorine or 200 ppm quaternary ammonium. Sponges shall not be used on food-contact surfaces. Except for fabric implements, linen shall not be used in contact with food. (114099.6, 114135, 114185.1, 114185.3 (d-e)). # 44a PREMISES KEPT FREE OF UNUSED EQUIPMENT
OBSERVATIONS: "True" brand reach in merchandiser is no longer functional. Remove or replace within 30 days. CHRONIC REPEAT VIOLATION:
Single deep fryer in cook's line is non functional. Correct or remove from premises within 30 days.
CODE DESCRIPTION: The premises of a food facility shall be kept free of items that are unnecessary to the operation or maintenance of the facility, such as equipment that is nonfunctional or no longer used. (114257.1.)
