Items listed on this report as violations do not meet the requirements set forth in the California Health and Safety Code commencing with section 7; 113700. # 6 HANDWASHING FACILITIES
OBSERVATIONS: MAJOR VIOLATION -Both hand wash stations at the bar lack soap. Correct today.
MINOR VIOLATION -Hand wash station by the prep back prep sink was partially blocked. Corrected.
CODE DESCRIPTION: Handwashing facilities shall be provided within or adjacent to toilet rooms, food preparation, and warewashing areas. Handwashing facilities shall be clean, unobstructed, and accessible at all times. Handwashing facilities shall be provided with soap and sanitary single-use towels or heated-air hand drying device in approved dispensers. Dispensers shall be maintained in good repair. (113953, 113953.1, 113953.2)

MAJOR

# 1c FOOD HANDLER CARDS
OBSERVATIONS: -Observed one expired food handler card for current employee. Correct within 30 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food handlers that prepare, handle, or serve non-prepackaged potentially hazardous food, shall obtain a valid Food Handler Card within 30 days after date of hire. (113948)
# 4 EATING, DRINKING, OR TOBACCO USE
OBSERVATIONS: -Observed one employee drink with lid and straw at the sushi prep table and one employee drink (open can) on the shelf inside the walkin refrigerator. Correct immediately.
CODE DESCRIPTION: Employees shall not eat, drink, or use tobacco in any area where contamination of food, clean equipment and utensils, linens, and single use articles may occur. Employees may drink from closed beverage container if the container is handled to prevent contamination of the employee's hands, the container, nonprepackaged food, and food-contact surfaces. Area designated for employees to eat and drink shall be located so that food, clean equipment and utensils, linens, and single-use articles are protected from contamination. (113977, 114256 (a))
# 7 HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES
OBSERVATIONS: -Observed one plastic container storing tofu at 42F-47F directly above ice by the soda machine. Correct immediately.
CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return to the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)
# 8 TIME AS A PUBLIC HEALTH CONTROL
OBSERVATIONS: -Observed cooked onions at the far left sushi bar using "time as a public health control" lacking tracking time on sticker. Corrected during inspection.
CODE DESCRIPTION: When time as a public health control is used, the following procedures shall be observed: food items shall be marked to indicate four hours past the time when the food was removed from temperature control, food shall be served or discarded within the four-hour time limit, written procedures for food that was prepared, cooked, and refrigerated before time as a public health control was used, shall be maintained at the food facility and be made available to the enforcement agency upon request. (114000)

# 30a FOOD STORED IN APPROVED LOCATION
OBSERVATIONS: -Observed one container of pickled ginger and one container of edamame stored directly on the floor inside the walkin refrigerator. Correct immediately.
CODE DESCRIPTION: Food items shall be stored at least six inches above the floor in an approved location within the fully enclosed permitted food facility. Food shall not be exposed to splash, dust, vermin, or other forms of contamination or adulteration. Prepackaged food may not be stored in direct contact with ice if the food is subject to the entry of water because of the nature of it's packaging. Non-prepackaged food may not be stored in direct contact with undrained ice. (114047, 114049, 114053, 114055, 114069 (b))
# 33 NONFOOD-CONTACT SURFACES CLEAN
OBSERVATIONS: Observed grease and/or dust and/or old food debris/residual in the following areas: -in plastic containers storing clean utensils -on sides and walls of flat grill and stove -underside of metal shelf on the stove -fire extinguisher by the stove -front portion of the "Wolf" oven
Clean and maintain all above areas and equipment within 7 days.
CODE DESCRIPTION: Nonfood-contact surfaces of equipment shall be kept free of an accumulation of dust, dirt, food residue, and other debris. Interior premises of a food facility shall be kept clean. (114115 (b), 114257)
# 35 EQUIPMENT APPROVED AND MAINTAINED
OBSERVATIONS: -Observed 6 raw blocks of wood storing below the three compartment sink. Remove from facility or reseal to ensure that blocks of wood are non absorbent and easily cleanable. Correct 48 hours.
-Dishwasher dispensing hot water at a maximum of 120F. Per Ecolab sticker displayed at the front, minimum hot water must be maintained at 125F at all times. Correct within 48 hours.
CODE DESCRIPTION: Utensils and equipment shall be kept fully operative and in good repair. All new and replacement equipment shall be properly installed and be certified by NSF, UL Sanitation, or ETL Sanitation. Facilities that wash, rinse, soak, thaw, or similarly prepare foods shall be provided with a approved food preparation sink. Surfaces such as cutting blocks and boards that are subject to scratching and scoring shall be resurfaced or replaced if they can no longer be effectively cleaned and sanitized. (114130, 114130.1, 114130.2, 114130.3, 114130.4, 114130.5, 114130.6, 114132, 114133, 114137, 114139, 114153, 114163, 114165, 114169, 114175, 114177, 114180)
# 36 EQUIPMENT STORED IN APPROVED LOCATION
OBSERVATIONS: -Observed 4 unsecured pressurized tanks at the bar station by the three compartment sink. Correct today.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Clean equipment, utensils, linens, and single-use articles shall be stored at least six inches above the floor in an approved location within the fully enclosed permitted food facility. Clean equipment, utensils, linens, and single-use articles shall not be exposed to splash, dust, vermin or other forms of contamination. Non-food items shall be stored and displayed separate from food and food-contact surfaces. During pauses in food preparation utensils shall be stored with handles above the top of the food, on a clean sanitized surface, in running water, or in water that is at least 135F. Single-use articles and multiservice utensils shall be handled, displayed, and dispensed so that contamination of food and lip-contact surfaces is prevented. Pressurized cylinders shall be securely fastened to rigid structure. (114074, 114081, 114119, 114121, 114161, 114172, 114178, 114179, 114083, 114185, 114185.2, 114185.3, 114185.4, 114185.5)

# 39 TEMPERATURE MEASURING DEVICES
OBSERVATIONS: -Monitoring thermometer for the far right reachin refrigerator at the sushi station was stored in the back of the unit with containers of food blocking thermometer from view. Ensure that monitoring thermometers are stored at the warmest section of the unit and in a manner that is easily viewable. Corrected. Monitoring thermometer was relocated to the front of the unit.
-Monitoring thermometer for the two door reachin refrigerator at the corner of the sushi station is incorrectly calibrated. Replace or repair within 7 days.
CODE DESCRIPTION: An accurate easily readable metal probe thermometer suitable for measuring temperature of food shall be available to the food handler. Thermometers shall be calibrated in accordance with manufacturer's specifications as necessary to ensure their accuracy. A thermometer shall be provided for all refrigerators, hot and cold holding units, and high temperature warewashing machines. (114157, 114159)
# 44e VERMIN AND ANIMAL EXCLUSION
OBSERVATIONS: -Observed the back door propped open upon arrival. Corrected during inspection.

CODE DESCRIPTION: A food facility shall at all times be constructed, equipped, and maintained as to prevent the entrance and harborage of animals, birds, and vermin, including, but not limited to, rodents and insects. Doors opening to outside air shall have a self-closing device and have no gaps greater than 1/4 inch when closed. Insect control devices shall be installed so that the devices are not located over a food or utensil handling areas. Pass-thru windows shall be of an approved size and be equipped with a self-closing device and air curtain if required. (114259, 114259.2, 114259.3)
# 45 FLOOR, WALL, AND CEILING FINISHES
OBSERVATIONS: -Observed heavy dust accumulation inside ceiling vent above the dish washer. Ceiling vent also lacks cover. Clean and maintain and provide ventilation cover within 7 days.
-Observed a hole in the wall approximately 5 inch by 5 inch wide behind the second hand sink from the right. Repair within 14 days.
CODE DESCRIPTION: Food facilities shall be fully enclosed in a building consisting of permanent floors, walls, and an overhead structure that meets minimum standards. The walls and ceiling shall be smooth, durable, nonabsorbent, and easily cleanable. Flooring and base coving shall be smooth, durable, and made of approved nonabsorbent material that is easily cleanable. Floor surfaces shall be coved at the juncture of the floor and wall with a 3/8 inch minimum radius and shall extend up the wall at least 4 inches. (114143 (d), 114266, 114268, 114268.1, 114271, 114272)
# 47c INSPECTION REPORT AVAILABILITY
OBSERVATIONS: -Most current inspection report not available during time of inspection. Ensure that the most current inspection report is available at all times during business hours. Correct today.
CODE DESCRIPTION: A copy of the most recent routine inspection report conducted to assess compliance with this part shall be maintained at the food facility and made available upon request. The food facility shall post a notice advising consumers that a copy of the most recent routine inspection report is available for review by any interested party. (113725.1)
