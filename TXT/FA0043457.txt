# 7 HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES
OBSERVATIONS: Major violations:

- Observed chicken tamales (125-127f) & pork tamales (123-127f) to be at improper holding temperatures inside of tamale warmer unit at front of restaurant.

- Observed pan of cooked pork (124-131f) to be at improper holding temperature on flat grill.

- Observed over-portioned cuts of cooked pork at front steam table.

Maintain all potentially hazardous foods (PHFs) at/below 41f or at/above 135f at all times. Corrected on site, as the tamale warmer unit was turned up to allow for the chicken and pork tamales to get to 135f after just being placed on tamale warmer within past 35 minutes. The pan of cooked pork on flat grill was reheated to 165f. The extra-portioned cuts of cooked pork at front steam table were reheated and placed inside a separate pan inside of front steam table.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed

containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return

to the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

# 4 EATING, DRINKING, OR TOBACCO USE

OBSERVATIONS: Open can of soda on prep table next to upright two door refrigeration unit. Corrected on site.

MAJOR

CODE DESCRIPTION: Employees shall not eat, drink, or use tobacco in any area where contamination of food, clean equipment and utensils, linens, and single use articles may occur. Employees may drink from closed beverage container if the container is handled to prevent contamination of the employee's hands, the container, nonprepackaged food, and food-contact surfaces. Area designated for employees to eat and drink shall be located so that food, clean equipment and utensils, linens, and single-use articles are protected from contamination. (113977, 114256 (a))
# 9 COOLING METHODS
OBSERVATIONS: Observed a covered pan of cooked rice (48f) to be improperly cooling inside of upright two door refrigeration unit. When cooling any potentially hazardous foods (PHFs) do not cover food item until the food temperature has reached at least 41f. Corrected on site, as pan of cooked rice was made earlier this morning (3 hours ago) and allowed to cool to 41f.
CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be rapidly cooled from 135F to 70F within 2 hours, and then from 70F to 41F within 4 hours. Cooling shall be by one or more of the following methods: in shallow containers, separating food into smaller portions, adding ice as an ingredient, using an ice bath, stirring frequently, using rapid cooling equipment, or using containers that facilitate heat transfer. (114002, 114002.1)
# 35 EQUIPMENT APPROVED AND MAINTAINED
OBSERVATIONS: Greasy ventilation hood baffles. Clean within 1 week.
