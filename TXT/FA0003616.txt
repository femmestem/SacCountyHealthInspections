Items listed on this report as violations do not meet the requirements set forth in the California Health and Safety Code commencing with section 7; 113700. All violations must be corrected within specified timeframe. Violations that are classified as "Major" pose an immediate threat to public health and have the potential to cause foodborne illness. # 4 EATING, DRINKING, OR TOBACCO USE
OBSERVATIONS: Upon start of inspection observed an employee eating pizza in the rear preparation area. Employees must eat in non food preparation areas such as the front customer table. Corrected at time of inspection.
CODE DESCRIPTION: Employees shall not eat, drink, or use tobacco in any area where contamination of food, clean equipment and utensils, linens, and single use articles may occur. Employees may drink from closed beverage container if the container is handled to prevent contamination of the employee's hands, the container, nonprepackaged food, and food-contact surfaces. Area designated for employees to eat and drink shall be located so that food, clean equipment and utensils, linens, and single-use articles are protected from contamination. (113977, 114256 (a))
# 6 HANDWASHING FACILITIES
OBSERVATIONS: Paper towel dispenser in restroom is not dispensing a full amount of paper when press handle is used. Ensure an adequate amount of paper is dispensed.
CODE DESCRIPTION: Handwashing facilities shall be provided within or adjacent to toilet rooms, food preparation, and warewashing areas. Handwashing facilities shall be clean, unobstructed, and accessible at all times. Handwashing facilities shall be provided with soap and sanitary single-use towels or heated-air hand drying device in approved dispensers. Dispensers shall be maintained in good repair. (113953, 113953.1, 113953.2)
# 14a FOOD CONTACT SURFACES CLEANED AND SANITIZED
OBSERVATIONS: Found food scissors in the rear preparation room on top shelf with food residue build up. Wash, rinse, sanitize immediately.
CODE DESCRIPTION: Food contact surfaces, utensils, and equipment shall be cleaned and sanitized at the following times: before each use with different type of raw food of animal origin, when changing from raw food to ready to eat food, between uses with raw produce and potentially hazardous food, before using a thermometer, any time during the operation when contamination may have occurred. Food-contact surfaces and utensils shall be clean to sight and touch. (114113, 114115, 114117, 114125(b), 114141)
# 21 HOT AND COLD WATER SUPPLY
OBSERVATIONS: Hot water from the faucet at the 3 compartment ware wash sink measured at 116F. Provide a minimum water temperature of 120F. Correct within 24 hours. Education provided.
CODE DESCRIPTION: An adequate, protected, pressurized, potable supply of hot and cold water shall be provided at all times. Hot water shall be supplied at a minimum temperature of at least 120F when measured from the faucet. The temperature of water provided to handwashing facilities shall be at least 100F. Handwashing facilities equipped with auto run faucets shall have their water temperature set between 100F to 108F. (113953(c), 114101(d), 114189, 114192, 114192.1, 114195)
# 23 VERMIN AND ANIMAL CONTAMINATION
OBSERVATIONS: Found one dead adult German cockroach underneath the makeshift bed in the rear storage room. Remove dead cockroach immediately. No sign of infestation observed during routine inspection. NOTE: Facility was able to provide monthly pest control receipts and observed multiple monitoring traps throughout facility with no activity. Continue to monitor for live activity and remove monitoring traps as needed. Education provided.
CODE DESCRIPTION: A food facility shall at all times be equipped, maintained, and operated as to prevent the entrance and harborage of animals, birds, and vermin, including, but not limited to rodents and insects. (114259, 114259.1, 114259.4, 114259.5)

# 30b FOOD CONTAINERS LABELED
OBSERVATIONS: Bulk container of flower at the front pizza preparation area and 3 smaller bulk containers by ware wash area were not labeled. Label container using contents common name. Correct today.
CODE DESCRIPTION: Containers holding food or food ingredients that are removed from their original packaging shall be identified with the common name of the food. (114051)
# 33 NONFOOD-CONTACT SURFACES CLEAN
OBSERVATIONS: The following areas were observed to be unclean: -floor below the non refrigerated preparation area -dust and cob web accumulation in the shelves below the cashier area -floor behind the washing machine -shelves inside the walk in cooler Clean and maintain within 14 days.
CODE DESCRIPTION: Nonfood-contact surfaces of equipment shall be kept free of an accumulation of dust, dirt, food residue, and other debris. Interior premises of a food facility shall be kept clean. (114115 (b), 114257)
# 34 WAREWASHING FACILITIES AND TESTING METHODS
OBSERVATIONS: One of two sets of sanitizer test strips was water damaged. Corrected by disposing damaged test strips at time of inspection.
CODE DESCRIPTION: Facilities that prepare food shall be equipped with a manual warewashing sink that has at least three compartments with two integral metal drainboards. A two compartment sink that was installed before January 1, 1996, need not be replaced when used in approved manner. Sink compartments shall be large enough to accommodate immersion of the largest equipment and utensils used. Testing materials shall be provided to adequately measure the applicable sanitization method used during manual or mechanical warewashing. (113984(e), 114067(f,g), 114099, 114099.3, 114099.5, 114101(a), 114101.1, 114101.2, 114103, 114107, 114125)
# 35 EQUIPMENT APPROVED AND MAINTAINED
OBSERVATIONS: Found several cracked/damaged plastic lids used for the food items inside the walk in cooler, pizza cold top reach in, and larger hot wing lid inside pizza cold top reach in refrigerator. Replace any damaged food container within 24 hours.
CODE DESCRIPTION: Utensils and equipment shall be kept fully operative and in good repair. All new and replacement equipment shall be properly installed and be certified by NSF, UL Sanitation, or ETL Sanitation. Facilities that wash, rinse, soak, thaw, or similarly prepare foods shall be provided with a approved food preparation sink. Surfaces such as cutting blocks and boards that are subject to scratching and scoring shall be resurfaced or replaced if they can no longer be effectively cleaned and sanitized. (114130, 114130.1, 114130.2, 114130.3, 114130.4, 114130.5, 114130.6, 114132, 114133, 114137, 114139, 114153, 114163, 114165, 114169, 114175, 114177, 114180)
# 39 TEMPERATURE MEASURING DEVICES
OBSERVATIONS: Unable to locate a monitoring thermometer inside the pizza cold top reach in refrigerator. Provide a thermometer in the warmest section of unit. Correct today.
CODE DESCRIPTION: An accurate easily readable metal probe thermometer suitable for measuring temperature of food shall be available to the food handler. Thermometers shall be calibrated in accordance with manufacturer's specifications as necessary to ensure their accuracy. A thermometer shall be provided for all refrigerators, hot and cold holding units, and high temperature warewashing machines. (114157, 114159)

# 42 GARBAGE AND REFUSE DISPOSAL
OBSERVATIONS: Dumpster lids observed open. Keep closed when not in use. Correct today.

CODE DESCRIPTION: The exterior premises of a food facility shall be kept free of litter and rubbish. All food waste and rubbish shall be kept in nonabsorbent, durable, cleanable, leak-proof, and rodent-proof containers. Containers shall have close-fitting lids and be covered at all times. All waste must be removed and disposed of as frequently as necessary to prevent the development of objectionable odors and other conditions that attract or harbor insects or rodents. (114244, 114245, 114245.1, 114245.2, 114245.3, 114245.4, 114245.5, 114245.6, 114245.7, 114257)
# 44c MOP SINK AND JANITORIAL AREA
OBSERVATIONS: Unclean water observed in mop container by ware wash area. Dispose of waste water now.

CODE DESCRIPTION: At least one janitorial sink equipped with hot and cold water and a drain shall be provided and conveniently located for the cleaning of mops or similar wet floor cleaning tools and for the disposal of mop water and similar liquid waste. A room, area, or cabinet separated from food preparation, food storage, or warewashing shall be provided for the storage of cleaning equipment and supplies. After use, mops shall be placed in a position that allows them to air-dry without soiling walls, equipment, or supplies. (114279, 114281, 114282)
# 44e VERMIN AND ANIMAL EXCLUSION
OBSERVATIONS: Rear door lacks self closing device. Provide a self closing device within 14 days.
Front main door is propped open. Keep door closed when not in use to prevent insects/ vermin from entering facility. Corrected at time of inspection.
CODE DESCRIPTION: A food facility shall at all times be constructed, equipped, and maintained as to prevent the entrance and harborage of animals, birds, and vermin, including, but not limited to, rodents and insects. Doors opening to outside air shall have a self-closing device and have no gaps greater than 1/4 inch when closed. Insect control devices shall be installed so that the devices are not located over a food or utensil handling areas. Pass-thru windows shall be of an approved size and be equipped with a self-closing device and air curtain if required. (114259, 114259.2, 114259.3)
# 46 LIVING AND SLEEPING QUARTERS
OBSERVATIONS: A make shift bed has been built in one of what used to be a restroom. Remove bed within 14 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Living and sleeping quarters shall not be located in any room, or directly open into any room where food is prepared, stored, or sold. (114285, 114286)
