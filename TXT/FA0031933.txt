# 8 TIME AS A PUBLIC HEALTH CONTROL
OBSERVATIONS: 2 spicy breaded chicken wraps lacked time stamp. Per manager, all product was prepared at 9am. Time stamp provided for 1pm at the time of inspection. Corrected.
NOTE: Product measured 135F in hot holding case.
CODE DESCRIPTION: When time as a public health control is used, the following procedures shall be observed: food items shall be marked to indicate four hours past the time when the food was removed from temperature control, food shall be served or discarded within the four-hour time limit, written procedures for food that was prepared, cooked, and refrigerated before time as a public health control was used, shall be maintained at the food facility and be made available to the enforcement agency upon request. (114000) # 14a FOOD CONTACT SURFACES CLEANED AND SANITIZED
OBSERVATIONS: Old sticky debris observed on specialty beverage powder chutes. Clean within 24 hours.

MAJOR

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food contact surfaces, utensils, and equipment shall be cleaned and sanitized at the following times: before each use with different type of raw food of animal origin, when changing from raw food to ready to eat food, between uses with raw produce and potentially hazardous food, before using a thermometer, any time during the operation when contamination may have occurred. Food-contact surfaces and utensils shall be clean to sight and touch. (114113, 114115, 114117, 114125(b), 114141) # 33 NONFOOD-CONTACT SURFACES CLEAN
OBSERVATIONS: Floor beneath beverage station unclean with old food debris and dust. Clean within 7 days.
Slight dust accumulation observed on fan guards in walk in cooler. Clean within 7 days.
CODE DESCRIPTION: Nonfood-contact surfaces of equipment shall be kept free of an accumulation of dust, dirt, food residue, and other debris. Interior premises of a food facility shall be kept clean. (114115 (b), 114257) # 34 WAREWASHING FACILITIES AND TESTING METHODS
OBSERVATIONS: Quat sanitizer test strips expired. Replace within 7 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Facilities that prepare food shall be equipped with a manual warewashing sink that has at least three compartments with two integral metal drainboards. A two compartment sink that was installed before January 1, 1996, need not be replaced when used in approved manner. Sink compartments shall be large enough to accommodate immersion of the largest equipment and utensils used. Testing materials shall be provided to adequately measure the applicable sanitization method used during manual or mechanical warewashing. (113984(e), 114067(f,g), 114099, 114099.3, 114099.5, 114101(a), 114101.1, 114101.2, 114103, 114107, 114125)
# 35 EQUIPMENT APPROVED AND MAINTAINED
OBSERVATIONS: Dispenser pipe cut horizontally at milk dispenser. Cut at an angle within 24 hours.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Utensils and equipment shall be kept fully operative and in good repair. All new and replacement equipment shall be properly installed and be certified by NSF, UL Sanitation, or ETL Sanitation. Facilities that wash, rinse, soak, thaw, or similarly prepare foods shall be provided with a approved food preparation sink. Surfaces such as cutting blocks and boards that are subject to scratching and scoring shall be resurfaced or replaced if they can no longer be effectively cleaned and sanitized.
