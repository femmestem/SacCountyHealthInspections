# 7 HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES
OBSERVATIONS: The following items are stored out of proper holding temperature: -tempura shrimps on prep table next to fryer at 68F -raw tuna in cold top at 47F -sliced bamboo in cold top at 48F -cream cheese in cold top at 46F -raw chicken in 3-door reach-in cooler at 44F
Correct immediately. Tempura shrimps were relocated into 3-door reach-in cooler during inspection; per owner, shrimps were cooked 30-60 minutes prior to inspection.
CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return to the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

# 1c FOOD HANDLER CARDS
OBSERVATIONS: California food handler cards for employees could not be located for inspection; per Owner, cards are maintained off-site. Locate and retain food handler cards on-site for next routine health inspection.
CODE DESCRIPTION: Food handlers that prepare, handle, or serve non-prepackaged potentially hazardous food, shall obtain a valid Food Handler Card within 30 days after date of hire. (113948) # 14a FOOD CONTACT SURFACES CLEANED AND SANITIZED
OBSERVATIONS: Food debris is observed on a vegetable peeler stored on the wall-mounted utensil storage strip designated for clean utensils only. Corrected; peeler was placed in warewash sink during inspection.
CODE DESCRIPTION: Food contact surfaces, utensils, and equipment shall be cleaned and sanitized at the following times: before each use with different type of raw food of animal origin, when changing from raw food to ready to eat food, between uses with raw produce and potentially hazardous food, before using a thermometer, any time during the operation when contamination may have occurred. Food-contact surfaces and utensils shall be clean to sight and touch. (114113, 114115, 114117, 114125(b), 114141) # 16 SHELLSTOCK IDENTIFICATION TAGS
OBSERVATIONS: Shellfish tags are not kept in chronological order. Correct within 7 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Shellstock shall be obtained in containers that bear legible source identification tags. Shellstock tags shall remain attached to the container that they are received in until the container is empty. Shellstock tags shall be maintained at the food facility in chronological order correlating to the sale date for 90 calendar days from the date of harvest. The source of the shellstock on display shall be identified. Shellstock that are portioned or prepackaged shall have a copy of the corresponding shellstock tag. (114039, 114039.1, 114039.3, 114039.4)

Scoop handle is stored in contact with food inside ice bin and bulk panko bin. Store utensil outside of food storage container or with handle upright and away from food. Correct within 24 hours.
Clean cutting board are stored behind prep sink faucet exposed to potential over spray contamination. Corrected; cutting boards were relocated during inspection.
CODE DESCRIPTION: Clean equipment, utensils, linens, and single-use articles shall be stored at least six inches above the floor in an approved location within the fully enclosed permitted food facility. Clean equipment, utensils, linens, and single-use articles shall not be exposed to splash, dust, vermin or other forms of contamination. Non-food items shall be stored and displayed separate from food and food-contact surfaces. During pauses in food preparation utensils shall be stored with handles above the top of the food, on a clean sanitized surface, in running water, or in water that is at least 135F. Single-use articles and multiservice utensils shall be handled, displayed, and dispensed so that contamination of food and lip-contact surfaces is prevented. Pressurized cylinders shall be securely fastened to rigid structure. (114074, 114081, 114119, 114121, 114161, 114172, 114178, 114179, 114083, 114185, 114185.2, 114185.3, 114185.4, 114185.5)

# 40 WIPING CLOTHS STORAGE AND USE
OBSERVATIONS: Damp wipe cloths are stored in cook's line without quaternary ammonia sanitizer residual, 0 ppm measured. Correct today.
CODE DESCRIPTION: Wiping cloths used to clean food and nonfood-contact surfaces shall be used only once unless kept in clean water with a sanitizer solution of 100 ppm available chlorine or 200 ppm quaternary ammonium. Sponges shall not be used on food-contact surfaces. Except for fabric implements, linen shall not be used in contact with food. (114099.6, 114135, 114185.1, 114185.3 (d-e)).
# 42 GARBAGE AND REFUSE DISPOSAL
OBSERVATIONS: Dumpster lids are left open. Keep lids shut when not actively filling. Correct within 24 hours.

CODE DESCRIPTION: The exterior premises of a food facility shall be kept free of litter and rubbish. All food waste and rubbish shall be kept in nonabsorbent, durable, cleanable, leak-proof, and rodent-proof containers. Containers shall have close-fitting lids and be covered at all times. All waste must be removed and disposed of as frequently as necessary to prevent the development of objectionable odors and other conditions that attract or harbor insects or rodents. (114244, 114245, 114245.1, 114245.2, 114245.3, 114245.4, 114245.5, 114245.6, 114245.7, 114257)
