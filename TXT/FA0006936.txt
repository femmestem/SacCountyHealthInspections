# 17 SHELLSTOCK DISPLAY AND COMMINGLING
OBSERVATIONS: MAJOR VIOLATION - Open shelled oysters from potentially different tags were commingled within one container. There were not tags available on the container. Cease this practice of mixing different batches of shellfish (shucked or unshucked) at all times. Oysters were voluntarily discarded during the inspection.
Two unopened sacks of oysters from different lots were delivered with tags that were not attached to the sacks. Do not serve these oysters. Do not accept delivery of shellfish if there is no tags within the sacks. Per owner, the sacks of oysters will be returned to the vendor and another set will be delivered with tags in the sack or tied on to the sack. Submit documentation from the vendor stating these two bags were returned. Failure to submit this document will result in a re-inspection charged at least $268.
CODE DESCRIPTION: Except in accordance with an approved HACCP plan, molluscan shellfish life-support system display tanks shall not be used to display shellfish that are offered for human consumption. Food facility shall ensure that shellstock from one tagged or labeled container are not commingled with shellstock from another container with different certification numbers. Food facility shall be in compliance with Gulf Oyster warning seasonal requirements. (Title 17 CA Code of Regulations õ13675, 113707)

OBSERVATIONS: Plate of cooked shrimp was placed onto a plate and deeply stacked then placed in the small reach-in refrigerator to cool while covered. Cease this practice immediately. Do not overstack/overfill containers of food being cooled. Do not put a cover on any food item being cooled. Do not place a very hot item in such a small unit (the refrigerator will being to warm other cold food items). Correct immediately.

Container of fresh deep fried shrimp was covered and placed near the deep fryer. Any cooling food items must be actively cooled properly without a cover and placed in a refrigerator capable of being able to cool it with a a timely manner. Implement immediately.
CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be rapidly cooled from 135F to 70F within 2 hours, and then from 70F to 41F within 4 hours. Cooling shall be by one or more of the following methods: in shallow containers, separating food into smaller portions, adding ice as an ingredient, using an ice bath, stirring frequently, using rapid cooling equipment, or using containers that facilitate heat transfer. (114002, 114002.1)
# 14a FOOD CONTACT SURFACES CLEANED AND SANITIZED
OBSERVATIONS: Mold/slime was observed on the edge of the deflector plate inside the ice machine. Clean and sanitize this surface immediately. Provide regular cleaning for this surface and around around the ice chute inside the ice machine.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food contact surfaces, utensils, and equipment shall be cleaned and sanitized at the following times: before each use with different type of raw food of animal origin, when changing from raw food to ready to eat food, between uses with raw produce and potentially hazardous food, before using a thermometer, any time during the operation when contamination may have occurred. Food-contact surfaces and utensils shall be clean to sight and touch. (114113, 114115, 114117, 114125(b), 114141)
# 16 SHELLSTOCK IDENTIFICATION TAGS
OBSERVATIONS: Shellfish tags are being removed from the sacks before the content of the sacks are sold. Cease this practice immediately. Any shellfish (oysters, clams, mussels, etc.) observed within the facility must be accompanied with a shellfish tag. Any observed shellfish (regardless of amount) without a tag with the shellfish shall be cited as a MAJOR VIOLATION and such oysters will be discarded.
CODE DESCRIPTION: Shellstock shall be obtained in containers that bear legible source identification tags. Shellstock tags shall remain attached to the container that they are received in until the container is empty. Shellstock tags shall be maintained at the food facility in chronological order correlating to the sale date for 90 calendar days from the date of harvest. The source of the shellstock on display shall be identified. Shellstock that are portioned or prepackaged shall have a copy of the corresponding shellstock tag. (114039, 114039.1, 114039.3, 114039.4)
# 24 PERSON IN CHARGE PRESENT AND PERFORMS DUTIES
OBSERVATIONS: Two dead cockroaches on the glue traps were observed under the prep sink. Remove this item immediately. Provide more traps as deemed necessary.

CODE DESCRIPTION: A person in charge shall be present at the food facility during all hours of operation. Person in charge shall restrict unnecessary individuals from entering food prep areas and monitor self-service areas and food displays to prevent food and equipment contamination. (113945-113945.1, 113984.1, 114075)
# 26 THAWING METHODS
OBSERVATIONS: Frozen shrimp was stored on the counter next to the prep sink. Cease this practice immediately. Implement one of four approved thawing practices immediately.
