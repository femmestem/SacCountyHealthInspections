# 7 HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES
OBSERVATIONS: The following food items are stored out of proper holding temperature: -chicken salad in cook's line reach-in cooler at 45F -French toast dip with raw liquid egg in cook's line cold top at 46F -whipped butter in service line cold top at 48F
Corrected; food items were voluntarily discarded by Manager during inspection.
CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return to the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)
# 23 VERMIN AND ANIMAL CONTAMINATION
OBSERVATIONS: 3 dead cockroaches are observed in space behind the trash compartment in waitress service line. Remove dead cockroaches and disinfect contaminated area within 24 hours.
Note: Facility receives weekly pest control service from Steritech Pest Control. Pest control service reports from 9/14/15 and 9/22/15 indicate a dramatic decrease in vermin activity. No live cockroaches were observed at time of inspection. Continue with aggressive pest control service to eradicate vermin and prevent future infestation. Failure to maintain a vermin/rodent free facility may result in a facility closure. Additional reinspection(s) may be conducted to verify compliance.
CODE DESCRIPTION: A food facility shall at all times be equipped, maintained, and operated as to prevent the entrance and harborage of animals, birds, and vermin, including, but not limited to rodents and insects. (114259, 114259.1, 114259.4, 114259.5)
# 27 FOOD PROTECTED FROM CONTAMINATION
OBSERVATIONS: Open cartons of Goldfish crackers is stored in dry food storage room. Transfer food into container with tight fitting lid within 2 days.
CODE DESCRIPTION: Food shall be protected from contamination during transportation, storage, preparation, holding, and display. Food and condiments on display shall be protected from contamination by the use of dispensers, packaging, counter, service line, or sneeze guards that intercept a direct line between the consumer's mouth and the food being displayed. Only prepackaged non-potentially hazardous food or uncut produce may be displayed or sold outdoors if under overhead cover. Open-air barbecues shall be separated from public access. (113984 (a-f), 113986, 114060, 114067, 114069, 114077, 114089.1 (c), 114143 (c-e))
# 30a FOOD STORED IN APPROVED LOCATION
OBSERVATIONS: Box of soda syrup is stored directly on the floor below syrup storage rack. Corrected on-site.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food items shall be stored at least six inches above the floor in an approved location within the fully enclosed permitted food facility. Food shall not be exposed to splash, dust, vermin, or other forms of contamination or adulteration. Prepackaged food may not be stored in direct contact with ice if the food is subject to the entry of water because of the nature of it's packaging. Non-prepackaged food may not be stored in direct contact with undrained ice. (114047, 114049, 114053, 114055, 114069 (b))

# 33 NONFOOD-CONTACT SURFACES CLEAN
OBSERVATIONS: Heavy build-up of old food particle and grease is observed on wall and wall behind cooking equipment. In addition, same degree of build-up is observed in space between grills and counter top. Clean and maintain within 7 days. (REPEAT VIOLATION)
Excessive slime and mold-like growth are observed on plumbing and other components underneath the warewash table and dish machine. Clean and maintain within 7 days.
CODE DESCRIPTION: Nonfood-contact surfaces of equipment shall be kept free of an accumulation of dust, dirt, food residue, and other debris. Interior premises of a food facility shall be kept clean. (114115 (b), 114257)
# 35 EQUIPMENT APPROVED AND MAINTAINED
OBSERVATIONS: 3 metal ladles stored on the clean dish storage racks are damaged. Corrected; damaged utensils were voluntarily discarded by Manager during inspection.
Small plastic cup is used as a scoop inside the container storing bulk sugar powder. Provide scoop with handle within 7 days.
CODE DESCRIPTION: Utensils and equipment shall be kept fully operative and in good repair. All new and replacement equipment shall be properly installed and be certified by NSF, UL Sanitation, or ETL Sanitation. Facilities that wash, rinse, soak, thaw, or similarly prepare foods shall be provided with a approved food preparation sink. Surfaces such as cutting blocks and boards that are subject to scratching and scoring shall be resurfaced or replaced if they can no longer be effectively cleaned and sanitized. (114130, 114130.1, 114130.2, 114130.3, 114130.4, 114130.5, 114130.6, 114132, 114133, 114137, 114139, 114153, 114163, 114165, 114169, 114175, 114177, 114180)
# 45 FLOOR, WALL, AND CEILING FINISHES
OBSERVATIONS: Floor tiles located behind cooking equipment in cook's line and in janitorial area are missing grout. Re-grout within 15 days. (REPEAT VIOLATION)
Small holes are observed on wall located left of back door. Seal holes within 30 days.
1 missing cove base tile is observed below the walk-in refrigerator door. Replace damaged tile within 14 days.
CODE DESCRIPTION: Food facilities shall be fully enclosed in a building consisting of permanent floors, walls, and an overhead structure that meets minimum standards. The walls and ceiling shall be smooth, durable, nonabsorbent, and easily cleanable. Flooring and base coving shall be smooth, durable, and made of approved nonabsorbent material that is easily cleanable. Floor surfaces shall be coved at the juncture of the floor and wall with a 3/8 inch minimum radius and shall extend up the wall at least 4 inches. (114143 (d), 114266, 114268, 114268.1, 114271, 114272)
