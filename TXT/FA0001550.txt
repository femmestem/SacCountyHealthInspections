Items listed on this report as violations do not meet the requirements set forth in the California Health and Safety Code commencing with section 7; 113700. # 7 HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES
OBSERVATIONS: Major Violation -Observed spring rolls storing above prep table at 74F-76F. Per operator, spring rolls were prepared at 10:30am (30 minutes prior). Operator states that "time as a public health control (TPHC)" will be used for all spring rolls in the future. Ensure that either a log or stickers with "prepared time" and "disposal time" are maintained. Potentially hazardous food must be discarded after a maximum of 4 hours from preparation if not sold. Food product may not be saved for later service if TPHC is used.
CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return to the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

MAJOR

# 1c FOOD HANDLER CARDS
OBSERVATIONS: -All three current employees food handler cards expired in July 2015. Correct within 30 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food handlers that prepare, handle, or serve non-prepackaged potentially hazardous food, shall obtain a valid Food Handler Card within 30 days after date of hire. (113948)
# 4 EATING, DRINKING, OR TOBACCO USE
OBSERVATIONS: -Observed two employee personal drinks (cups without lid and straw) stored above beverages for customers inside front beverage refrigerator. Corrected during inspection.
CODE DESCRIPTION: Employees shall not eat, drink, or use tobacco in any area where contamination of food, clean equipment and utensils, linens, and single use articles may occur. Employees may drink from closed beverage container if the container is handled to prevent contamination of the employee's hands, the container, nonprepackaged food, and food-contact surfaces. Area designated for employees to eat and drink shall be located so that food, clean equipment and utensils, linens, and single-use articles are protected from contamination. (113977, 114256 (a))
# 14a FOOD CONTACT SURFACES CLEANED AND SANITIZED
OBSERVATIONS: -Observed one strainer with grease accumulation stored hanging from shelf where clean utensils are stored. Strainer was relocated to the three compartment sink for washing during time of inspection.
CODE DESCRIPTION: Food contact surfaces, utensils, and equipment shall be cleaned and sanitized at the following times: before each use with different type of raw food of animal origin, when changing from raw food to ready to eat food, between uses with raw produce and potentially hazardous food, before using a thermometer, any time during the operation when contamination may have occurred. Food-contact surfaces and utensils shall be clean to sight and touch. (114113, 114115, 114117, 114125(b), 114141)
# 26 THAWING METHODS
OBSERVATIONS: -Observed two frozen 5 gallon buckets of beef broth thawing at room temperature on the prep table opposite the stoves. Ensure approved and proper thawing is completed as shown on thawing sticker posted at the beverage refrigerator. Correct today.
CODE DESCRIPTION: Frozen foods shall be stored or displayed in their frozen state unless being thawed in the following manner: under refrigeration, completely submerged under cold running water with sufficient velocity to flush loose particles, in microwave oven, during the cooking process. (114018, 114020)

# 27 FOOD PROTECTED FROM CONTAMINATION
OBSERVATIONS: -Observed several opened cans and bottles of liquid seasoning storing on the shelf to the left of the stove. Ensure all food product is covered at all times in between use. Correct today.
CODE DESCRIPTION: Food shall be protected from contamination during transportation, storage, preparation, holding, and display. Food and condiments on display shall be protected from contamination by the use of dispensers, packaging, counter, service line, or sneeze guards that intercept a direct line between the consumer's mouth and the food being displayed. Only prepackaged non-potentially hazardous food or uncut produce may be displayed or sold outdoors if under overhead cover. Open-air barbecues shall be separated from public access. (113984 (a-f), 113986, 114060, 114067, 114069, 114077, 114089.1 (c), 114143 (c-e))
# 33 NONFOOD-CONTACT SURFACES CLEAN
OBSERVATIONS: Observed grease and/or dust and/or old food debris and/or food/sauce residual at the following areas in the kitchen: -fan guards in the upright true refrigerator -shelf storing spices and sauces left of stove -sides, behind and below all cooking equipment under the ventilation hood -below both upright refrigerators -below both the two and three compartment sink -below shelves connecting the two and three compartment sink -on the shelf storing spices opposite the deep fryer -on plastic cabinets storing spices below the prep table storing the meat slicer
Clean and maintain all above areas within 7 days.
CODE DESCRIPTION: Nonfood-contact surfaces of equipment shall be kept free of an accumulation of dust, dirt, food residue, and other debris. Interior premises of a food facility shall be kept clean. (114115 (b), 114257)
# 35 EQUIPMENT APPROVED AND MAINTAINED

-Observed one raw wood shelf (paint has deteriorated) used to store large pots and containers under the two compartment sink. Repair or replace within 7 days.
-Observed one cardboard box used to store vegetables inside the reachin refrigerator. Replace with a smooth cleanable container with cover within 7 days.
CODE DESCRIPTION: Utensils and equipment shall be kept fully operative and in good repair. All new and replacement equipment shall be properly installed and be certified by NSF, UL Sanitation, or ETL Sanitation. Facilities that wash, rinse, soak, thaw, or similarly prepare foods shall be provided with a approved food preparation sink. Surfaces such as cutting blocks and boards that are subject to scratching and scoring shall be resurfaced or replaced if they can no longer be effectively cleaned and sanitized.
