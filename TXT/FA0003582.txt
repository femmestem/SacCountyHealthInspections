# 1c FOOD HANDLER CARDS
OBSERVATIONS: Food handler cards expired for multiple employees. Provide current food handler cards for all employees within 8 days.
CODE DESCRIPTION: Food handlers that prepare, handle, or serve non-prepackaged potentially hazardous food, shall obtain a valid Food Handler Card within 30 days after date of hire. (113948)
# 7 HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES
OBSERVATIONS: Pooled eggs and pooled egg whites measured 48F stored on top of ice bath in cold-top inserts. Make sure ice is to the level of the product to keep it cold. Pooled eggs were relocated to reach-in and ice was added to cold-top where egg whites are stored.
CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return to the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)
# 35 EQUIPMENT APPROVED AND MAINTAINED
OBSERVATIONS: -Scoop used in rice lack handle. Provide scoop with handle and store with handle out of food items. Correct within 1 day.
-Old card board boxes in use to store food items in the dry food storage area. Use approved containers (easily cleanable, non-absorbent, smooth). Correct within 2 days.
CODE DESCRIPTION: Utensils and equipment shall be kept fully operative and in good repair. All new and replacement equipment shall be properly installed and be certified by NSF, UL Sanitation, or ETL Sanitation. Facilities that wash, rinse, soak, thaw, or similarly prepare foods shall be provided with a approved food preparation sink. Surfaces such as cutting blocks and boards that are subject to scratching and scoring shall be resurfaced or replaced if they can no longer be effectively cleaned and sanitized.
