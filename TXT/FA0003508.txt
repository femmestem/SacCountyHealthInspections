# 7 HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES
OBSERVATIONS: MAJOR VIOLATION - Egg wash (63F) at the cook's line handwash station was out of proper holding temperature. Maintain all potentially hazardous foods (PHF) at proper holding temperature at all times. This item was voluntarily discarded.

MINOR VIOLATION - Potato curry (45F), chicken curry (45F) in the makeline top insert across the stove and seared ahi (47F) in the sushi case were out of proper holding temperature. Correct immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be

held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return

to the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

# 1a DEMONSTRATION OF KNOWLEDGE

OBSERVATIONS: Staff was not aware of proper calibration method for thermometer and final cooking temperature for meats. Education was provided during the inspection to calibrate thermometers to 32F in heavily iced water and cook chicken (165F), ground meats (155F), beef, pork, eggs, seafood (145F) before serving.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food employees shall have adequate knowledge of food safety as it relates to their assigned duties. (113947)

# 1c FOOD HANDLER CARDS
OBSERVATIONS: Two food handler certificates were not available and one has expired. Register all current food handlers to an approved class within 30 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food handlers that prepare, handle, or serve non-prepackaged potentially hazardous food, shall obtain a valid Food Handler Card within 30 days after date of hire. (113948)
# 4 EATING, DRINKING, OR TOBACCO USE
OBSERVATIONS: Many employee beverages were observed without lids or straws. Provide lid AND straw to all employee beverages at all times. Correct within 24 hours.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Employees shall not eat, drink, or use tobacco in any area where contamination of food, clean equipment and utensils, linens, and single use articles may occur. Employees may drink from closed beverage container if the container is handled to prevent contamination of the employee's hands, the container, nonprepackaged food, and food-contact surfaces. Area designated for employees to eat and drink shall be located so that food, clean equipment and utensils, linens, and single-use articles are protected from contamination. (113977, 114256 (a))
# 9 COOLING METHODS
OBSERVATIONS: Containers of cooked sliced chicken being cooled were stored with a cover. Do not cover food items engaged in the cooling process. Implement immediately.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be rapidly cooled from 135F to 70F within 2 hours, and then from 70F to 41F within 4 hours. Cooling shall be by one or more of the following methods: in shallow containers, separating food into smaller portions, adding ice as an ingredient, using an ice bath, stirring frequently, using rapid cooling equipment, or using containers that facilitate heat transfer. (114002, 114002.1)

# 14a FOOD CONTACT SURFACES CLEANED AND SANITIZED
OBSERVATIONS: Mold/slime accumulation observed on the underside of the ice deflector inside the ice machine. Clean and sanitize this surface within 3 days.
CODE DESCRIPTION: Food contact surfaces, utensils, and equipment shall be cleaned and sanitized at the following times: before each use with different type of raw food of animal origin, when changing from raw food to ready to eat food, between uses with raw produce and potentially hazardous food, before using a thermometer, any time during the operation when contamination may have occurred. Food-contact surfaces and utensils shall be clean to sight and touch. (114113, 114115, 114117, 114125(b), 114141)
# 16 SHELLSTOCK IDENTIFICATION TAGS
OBSERVATIONS: Shellfish tags were not arranged in chronological order. Store these tags by types of shellfish and in chronological order at all times. Correct within 7 days.
CODE DESCRIPTION: Shellstock shall be obtained in containers that bear legible source identification tags. Shellstock tags shall remain attached to the container that they are received in until the container is empty. Shellstock tags shall be maintained at the food facility in chronological order correlating to the sale date for 90 calendar days from the date of harvest. The source of the shellstock on display shall be identified. Shellstock that are portioned or prepackaged shall have a copy of the corresponding shellstock tag. (114039, 114039.1, 114039.3, 114039.4)
# 23 VERMIN AND ANIMAL CONTAMINATION
OBSERVATIONS: Dead roaches were observed in the vermin traps under the sushi case, under the microwave near the ice machine, by the bento box above the handwash station. Change the traps and clean these areas to determine if there is any recent activity within the facility. Correct within 3 days.
NOTE: Pest control company provides service every two weeks. Last service was on 10/29/15.
CODE DESCRIPTION: A food facility shall at all times be equipped, maintained, and operated as to prevent the entrance and harborage of animals, birds, and vermin, including, but not limited to rodents and insects. (114259, 114259.1, 114259.4, 114259.5)
# 27 FOOD PROTECTED FROM CONTAMINATION
OBSERVATIONS: Raw food items were stored above/behind ready to eat items various refrigeration units. Store all raw food items accordingly to prevent cross contamination. From top to bottom or rear to front ---> Ready to eat foods (cooked foods, food not requiring cooking), raw eggs, raw seafood, raw pork/beef, raw ground pork/beef, raw poultry. Correct immediately.
Open bags of panko on the rack by the back area. Store all open packages of food items in containers with tight fitting lid at all times. Correct within 7 days.
Food items for the employees (also served to customers), placed on the counter near the 3-comp sink were unattended. Provide adequate label identifying food is for employees. Failure to comply will subject such food items to regulations. Correct immediately.
Container of salmon remain on the floor in the sushi area was unattended while preparing orders. Provide cover to the container to maintain proper holding temperature or place in refrigerator if it is not ready to be prepared. Correct immediately.

# 29 IDENTIFICATION AND STORAGE OF TOXIC SUBSTANCES
OBSERVATIONS: Many spray bottles of chemicals were lacking labels. Provide labels to all containers with chemicals within 7 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: All poisonous substances, detergents, sanitizers, and cleaning compounds shall be stored to prevent contamination of food, equipment, utensils, linens, and single-use articles. Only pesticides that are necessary and specifically approved for use in a food facility may be used. Containers of poisonous or toxic materials shall have a legible manufacturer's label or if taken from bulk supplies shall be clearly and individually identified with the common name of the material. (114254, 114254.1, 114254.2)
# 30a FOOD STORED IN APPROVED LOCATION
OBSERVATIONS: Container of fish was stored on the sushi area floor then transferred to the walk-in floor. Store all food and food related items (especially in the storage room) at least 6" off the floor at all times.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food items shall be stored at least six inches above the floor in an approved location within the fully enclosed permitted food facility. Food shall not be exposed to splash, dust, vermin, or other forms of contamination or adulteration. Prepackaged food may not be stored in direct contact with ice if the food is subject to the entry of water because of the nature of it's packaging. Non-prepackaged food may not be stored in direct contact with undrained ice. (114047, 114049, 114053, 114055, 114069 (b))
# 30b FOOD CONTAINERS LABELED
OBSERVATIONS: Re-used buckets with various flours near the deep fryer were lacking labels. Provide labels to all containers with food within 7 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Containers holding food or food ingredients that are removed from their original packaging shall be identified with the common name of the food. (114051)
# 33 NONFOOD-CONTACT SURFACES CLEAN
OBSERVATIONS: Debris accumulation observed on the floor under the refrigeration units right of the dishwasher. Clean floor within 3 days.

Spilled animal blood was observed at the bottom of the upright freezer in the storage room. Clean freezer within 7 days.
CODE DESCRIPTION: Nonfood-contact surfaces of equipment shall be kept free of an accumulation of dust, dirt, food residue, and other debris. Interior premises of a food facility shall be kept clean. (114115 (b), 114257)
# 34 WAREWASHING FACILITIES AND TESTING METHODS
OBSERVATIONS: pH strips for testing sushi rice was not available. Provide test strips capable to proving food items are 4.6 or below at all times. Correct within 7 days.

# 35 EQUIPMENT APPROVED AND MAINTAINED
OBSERVATIONS: Domestic grade crockpot (for warming teriyaki sauce) and blender observed in the back kitchen area. Remove these unapproved equipment within 4 months. All equipment must be commercial grade and bear an NSF or equivalent decal at all times.

Refrigeration unit across the stove was observed with an ambient temperature of 49F. Adjust thermostat to maintain ambient temperature at 41F or below at all times. Correct immediately.
CODE DESCRIPTION: Utensils and equipment shall be kept fully operative and in good repair. All new and replacement equipment shall be properly installed and be certified by NSF, UL Sanitation, or ETL Sanitation. Facilities that wash, rinse, soak, thaw, or similarly prepare foods shall be provided with a approved food preparation sink. Surfaces such as cutting blocks and boards that are subject to scratching and scoring shall be resurfaced or replaced if they can no longer be effectively cleaned and sanitized. (114130, 114130.1, 114130.2, 114130.3, 114130.4, 114130.5, 114130.6, 114132, 114133, 114137, 114139, 114153, 114163, 114165, 114169, 114175, 114177, 114180)
# 36 EQUIPMENT STORED IN APPROVED LOCATION
OBSERVATIONS: Used utensils are stored in stagnant water (77-92F) in the kitchen. All used utensils must be stored in a manner protected from bacterial growth at all times. Correct immediately.

Handle of some scoops (especially for the flours) were in contact with the food item. Store utensils in a manner protected from contamination. Correct immediately.
CODE DESCRIPTION: Clean equipment, utensils, linens, and single-use articles shall be stored at least six inches above the floor in an approved location within the fully enclosed permitted food facility. Clean equipment, utensils, linens, and single-use articles shall not be exposed to splash, dust, vermin or other forms of contamination. Non-food items shall be stored and displayed separate from food and food-contact surfaces. During pauses in food preparation utensils shall be stored with handles above the top of the food, on a clean sanitized surface, in running water, or in water that is at least 135F. Single-use articles and multiservice utensils shall be handled, displayed, and dispensed so that contamination of food and lip-contact surfaces is prevented. Pressurized cylinders shall be securely fastened to rigid structure. (114074, 114081, 114119, 114121, 114161, 114172, 114178, 114179, 114083, 114185, 114185.2, 114185.3, 114185.4, 114185.5)
# 38 VENTILATION AND LIGHTING
OBSERVATIONS: Hood filters above the grill are in need of cleaning. Clean within 7 days.

Two of three hood filters by the noodle area are being lifted. Adjust to allow hood filters to remain flat to prevent fire hazard due to accumulation of grease vapors behind the filters. Correct within 7 days.

Light fixture inside the refrigerator at the end of the middle section of the cook's line (facing the makeline for the ramen area) was not functional. Repair light fixture within 7 days.

# 39 TEMPERATURE MEASURING DEVICES
OBSERVATIONS: Probe thermometer could not be located in the kitchen. Provide probe thermometer capable of measuring between 0F to 220F within 7 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: An accurate easily readable metal probe thermometer suitable for measuring temperature of food shall be available to the food handler. Thermometers shall be calibrated in accordance with manufacturer's specifications as necessary to ensure their accuracy. A thermometer shall be provided for all refrigerators, hot and cold holding units, and high temperature warewashing machines. (114157, 114159)
# 41 PLUMBING FIXTURES AND BACKFLOW PREVENTION
OBSERVATIONS: Drain pipe from the ice machine is lacking a 1" air gap. Provide air gap within 3 days.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Steam tables, ice machines, ice bins, food preparation sinks, display cases, walk-in refrigeration units, and other similar equipment that discharge liquid waste shall drain into a floor sink with an air gap or other approved type of receptor. Plumbing and plumbing fixtures shall be maintained so as to prevent contamination, shall be kept clean, fully operative, and in good repair. The potable water supply shall be protected with a backflow or back siphonage protection device. Any hose used for potable water shall be of approved materials, labeled, properly stored, and used for no other purpose. (114171, 114190, 114192, 114193, 114193.1, 114199, 114201, 114269)
# 42 GARBAGE AND REFUSE DISPOSAL
OBSERVATIONS: Community shared dumpster had open lids. Keep lids closed at all times. Correct within 24 hours.

CODE DESCRIPTION: The exterior premises of a food facility shall be kept free of litter and rubbish. All food waste and rubbish shall be kept in nonabsorbent, durable, cleanable, leak-proof, and rodent-proof containers. Containers shall have close-fitting lids and be covered at all times. All waste must be removed and disposed of as frequently as necessary to prevent the development of objectionable odors and other conditions that attract or harbor insects or rodents. (114244, 114245, 114245.1, 114245.2, 114245.3, 114245.4, 114245.5, 114245.6, 114245.7, 114257)
# 44d EMPLOYEE PERSONAL ITEMS
OBSERVATIONS: Many employee items (cell phone, beverages, aprons, hats) were store directly on top of or above food and food related items for the facility were observed. Store all personal items under or away from all food and food related items for the facility. Correct immediately.
CODE DESCRIPTION: No person shall store clothing or personal effects in any area used for the storage and preparation of food. Lockers or other suitable facilities shall be provided and used for the orderly storage of employee clothing and other possessions. Lockers or other suitable facilities shall be located in a designated room or area where contamination of food, equipment, utensils, and single-use articles cannot occur. (114256, 114256.1.)
