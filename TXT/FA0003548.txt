# 14b WAREWASHING AND SANITIZING PROCEDURES

OBSERVATIONS: Main kitchen dish washer observed not dispensing sanitizer (0 ppm chlorine) and in use at time of inspection. Sanitizer bottle observed empty at time of inspection. New sanitizer bottle was available. Corrected during inspection. Test machine daily before use to ensure a minimum of 50 ppm chlorine is present. 2ND REPEAT VIOLATION.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food-contact surfaces and multiservice utensils shall be effectively washed, rinsed, and sanitized to remove or completely loosen soils by use of manual or mechanical methods. The temperature of the washing solution shall be maintained at least 100F or at the temperature specified by the manufacturer's label. Manual sanitation shall be accomplished during the final rinse by contact with solution containing 100 ppm available chlorine or 200 ppm quaternary ammonium. Mechanical sanitization shall be accomplished in the final rinse by achieving a utensil surface temperature of 160F or by contact with solution containing 50 ppm available chlorine. After cleaning and sanitizing, equipment and utensils shall be air dried. (114097, 114099.1, 114099.2, 114099.4, 114099.6, 114099.7, 114101 (b-d), 114105, 114109, 114111)

# 7 HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES

OBSERVATIONS: The following potentially hazardous food items were stored at improper holding temperature:

Stored on steam table - one tray of baked potatoes measured at 110F-120F - one tray of mashed potatoes measured at 110F-120F

Per chef, noted food items above were made this morning. Observed temperature of the steam table measured at 144F; chef raised the temperature to higher setting. Food items were reheated to 165F during inspection. Monitor to ensure food temperature stays/above 135F at all times.

MAJOR

- Observed one container of cut tomatoes measured at 44F, one container of shredded cheese measured at 50F. Food items were kept on ice. Ice was not equal to the product in containers. Ensure ice is equal to the level of products in bowls. Keep all potentially hazardous food at/below 41F for cold food and at/above 135F for hot food. Correct today.
- Observed one container of cut watermelon stored above fill line in cold top cooler at main cook's line observed at 39F-47F. Keep cold top lids closed when not in use and keep inserts only 3/4 full to help maintain temperature.
CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return to the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)
