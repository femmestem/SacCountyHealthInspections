# 8 TIME AS A PUBLIC HEALTH CONTROL
OBSERVATIONS: Chicken fries at hot holding unit: Timer on one pan of fries read the expiration time 7:30 pm, which is more than 4 hours from the time of inspection. On the other container of chicken fries start time read 2:30 pm (expiration time 3pm written), but the fries were cooked before that (checked during inspection at 2:30pm).
According to manager chicken fries are good for 30 minutes only.
Track time properly, mark down the start time right away after placing them at hot holding.
Discarded voluntarily during inspection.
CODE DESCRIPTION: When time as a public health control is used, the following procedures shall be observed: food items shall be marked to indicate four hours past the time when the food was removed from temperature control, food shall be served or discarded within the four-hour time limit, written procedures for food that was prepared, cooked, and refrigerated before time as a public health control was used, shall be maintained at the food facility and be made available to the enforcement agency upon request. (114000) # 14a FOOD CONTACT SURFACES CLEANED AND SANITIZED
OBSERVATIONS: Shelving rack in walkin refrigerator (under the cooling unit) unclean. Clean within 3 days and maintain.

MAJOR

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food contact surfaces, utensils, and equipment shall be cleaned and sanitized at the following times: before each use with different type of raw food of animal origin, when changing from raw food to ready to eat food, between uses with raw produce and potentially hazardous food, before using a thermometer, any time during the operation when contamination may have occurred. Food-contact surfaces and utensils shall be clean to sight and touch. (114113, 114115, 114117, 114125(b), 114141)
