# 7 HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES
OBSERVATIONS: Observed one pre-made cheese pizza measured at 64F (non baked) being stored on prep table without temperature control. Per manager, pizza was made 20 minutes prior to inspection. All potentially hazardous food items must be held at proper temperature or held using time as a public health control (TPHC). If TPHC is implemented, facility must mark the items to show the time 4 hours past the time it was taken out of temperature control and must be used or discarded. Corrected by relocating the noted item to walk in cooler during inspection.

Note: TPHC handout provided during inspection.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be

held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return to the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

# 1c FOOD HANDLER CARDS

OBSERVATIONS: Proof of current food handler card for two employees was not available during inspection. Correct within 30 days.

Note: "List of approved and accredited food safety certification examinations" handout provided during inspection.
CODE DESCRIPTION: Food handlers that prepare, handle, or serve non-prepackaged potentially hazardous food, shall obtain a valid Food Handler Card within 30 days after date of hire. (113948) # 34 WAREWASHING FACILITIES AND TESTING METHODS
OBSERVATIONS: Sanitizer test strips for quaternary ammonium was not available. Correct within 7 days.

Note: "Restaurant supplies and equipment" handout provided during inspection.
CODE DESCRIPTION: Facilities that prepare food shall be equipped with a manual warewashing sink that has at least three compartments with two integral metal drainboards. A two compartment sink that was installed before January 1, 1996, need not be replaced when used in approved manner. Sink compartments shall be large enough to accommodate immersion of the largest equipment and utensils used. Testing materials shall be provided to adequately measure the applicable sanitization method used during manual or mechanical warewashing. (113984(e), 114067(f,g), 114099, 114099.3, 114099.5, 114101(a), 114101.1, 114101.2, 114103, 114107, 114125)
