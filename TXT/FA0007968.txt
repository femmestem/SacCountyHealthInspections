# 9 COOLING METHODS
OBSERVATIONS: Cooked polenta 73F-98F cooling improperly (about 5-6F deep metal containers) inside walk-in cooler. Per employee, polenta was cooked at 11am for about one hour, slightly cooled and then poured inside metal containers, held on push rack, and then placed inside walk-in cooler. Polenta was still above 90F at 3pm. Education provided. Polenta was voluntarily discarded during inspection.

Minor Violation: Found covered container with spaghetti 55F-64F(about 8 inch deep) cooling inside walk-in cooler. Per employee, spaghetti was boiled at about 12pm. Employee placed food inside uncovered shallow tray during inspection.

Foods must be cooled from 135F to 70F within 2 hours and from 70F to 41F within 4 hours using approved cooling methods. Approved methods include reducing portion size, use of shallow uncovered containers filled no higher than 2 inches, ice bath or ice wand. Once 41F is verified with a thermometer, foods may be combined into larger portions and covered.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be rapidly cooled from 135F to 70F within 2 hours, and then from 70F to 41F within 4 hours. Cooling shall be by one or more of the following methods: in shallow containers, separating food into smaller portions, adding ice

as an ingredient, using an ice bath, stirring frequently, using rapid cooling equipment, or using containers that facilitate heat transfer.

(114002, 114002.1)

Maintain all cold potentially hazardous foods at/below 41F at all times. REPEAT VIOLATION
CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return to the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food contact surfaces, utensils, and equipment shall be cleaned and sanitized at the following times: before each use with different type of raw food of animal origin, when changing from raw food to ready to eat food, between uses with raw produce and potentially hazardous food, before using a thermometer, any time during the operation when contamination may have occurred. # 26 THAWING METHODS
OBSERVATIONS: Observed frozen calamari and raw pork improperly thawing on push cart. Potentially hazardous food must be thawed in an approved method: under refrigeration, under cold running water while submerged in water, in microwave, or during the cooking process. Corrected during inspection. Thawing sticker was provided.
CODE DESCRIPTION: Frozen foods shall be stored or displayed in their frozen state unless being thawed in the following manner: under refrigeration, completely submerged under cold running water with sufficient velocity to flush loose particles, in microwave oven, during the cooking process. (114018, 114020)
# 27 FOOD PROTECTED FROM CONTAMINATION
OBSERVATIONS: - Raw pork stored on top of package with sliced ham, raw pork stored next to sausages and raw chicken, and creme sheets stored on top of raw fish inside 2-door upright freezer. Employee corrected during inspection. REPEAT VIOLATION

- Raw pork improperly defrosting on tray above cooked beets located on push cart. Corrected during inspection.
CODE DESCRIPTION: Food shall be protected from contamination during transportation, storage, preparation, holding, and display. Food and condiments on display shall be protected from contamination by the use of dispensers, packaging, counter, service line, or sneeze guards that intercept a direct line between the consumer's mouth and the food being displayed. Only prepackaged non-potentially hazardous food or uncut produce may be displayed or sold outdoors if under overhead cover. Open-air barbecues shall be separated from public access. (113984 (a-f), 113986, 114060, 114067, 114069, 114077, 114089.1 (c), 114143 (c-e))
# 33 NONFOOD-CONTACT SURFACES CLEAN
OBSERVATIONS: - Debris build up on the floor inside water heater room, beneath unused section of bar (between dish machine and handwash sink), behind pizza oven, and beneath ice machine. - Mold residue build up on four crates storing beer at walk-in cooler. - Old food particles build up on several shelves inside walk-in cooler. - Dust and debris observed on shelves storing soup cups and lids. Clean and maintain within 7 days.
CODE DESCRIPTION: Nonfood-contact surfaces of equipment shall be kept free of an accumulation of dust, dirt, food residue, and other debris. Interior premises of a food facility shall be kept clean. (114115 (b), 114257)

# 36 EQUIPMENT STORED IN APPROVED LOCATION
OBSERVATIONS: - Found two knives jammed between two reach-in coolers. Cease practice immediately. All equipment must be stored in hygienic manner. Corrected.
- Multi use utensils at grill station stored in stagnant water (74F). Store utensils at/above 135F, ice water (41F or below), or vinegar water with pH at/below 4.6. Corrected.
- Ice scoop stored inside unclean container on top of ice machine. Clean immediately.
CODE DESCRIPTION: Clean equipment, utensils, linens, and single-use articles shall be stored at least six inches above the floor in an approved location within the fully enclosed permitted food facility. Clean equipment, utensils, linens, and single-use articles shall not be exposed to splash, dust, vermin or other forms of contamination. Non-food items shall be stored and displayed separate from food and food-contact surfaces. During pauses in food preparation utensils shall be stored with handles above the top of the food, on a clean sanitized surface, in running water, or in water that is at least 135F. Single-use articles and multiservice utensils shall be handled, displayed, and dispensed so that contamination of food and lip-contact surfaces is prevented. Pressurized cylinders shall be securely fastened to rigid structure. (114074, 114081, 114119, 114121, 114161, 114172, 114178, 114179, 114083, 114185, 114185.2, 114185.3, 114185.4, 114185.5)
# 38 VENTILATION AND LIGHTING
OBSERVATIONS: Loose light guards observed above grill and pasta reach-in cooler. Secure within 7 days. REPEAT VIOLATION

CODE DESCRIPTION: Exhaust hoods shall be provided to remove toxic gases, heat, grease, vapors and smoke and be approved by health and building department. Canopy-type hoods shall extend six inches beyond all cooking equipment. All areas shall have sufficient ventilation to facilitate proper food storage. Toilet rooms shall be vented to the outside air by a screened openable window, air shaft, or light-switch activated exhaust fan. Adequate lighting shall be provided in all areas to facilitate cleaning and inspection. Light fixtures in areas where open food is stored, served, prepared, and where utensils are washed shall be of shatterproof construction or protected with light shields. (114149, 114149.1, 114149.2, 114149.3, 114252, 114252.1)
# 40 WIPING CLOTHS STORAGE AND USE
OBSERVATIONS: Found two soiled and slightly wet cloth towels without any sanitizer on pizza prep table. Store in sanitizer water 200 ppm quat ammonium when not in active use. Corrected.
CODE DESCRIPTION: Wiping cloths used to clean food and nonfood-contact surfaces shall be used only once unless kept in clean water with a sanitizer solution of 100 ppm available chlorine or 200 ppm quaternary ammonium. Sponges shall not be used on food-contact surfaces. Except for fabric implements, linen shall not be used in contact with food. (114099.6, 114135, 114185.1, 114185.3 (d-e)).
# 41 PLUMBING FIXTURES AND BACKFLOW PREVENTION
OBSERVATIONS: Soda dispenser drain pipes nesting on top of floor sink and ice machine condensation drains on the floor (loose plumbing located on the floor). Provide at least 1 inch air gap within 7 days.

# 44c MOP SINK AND JANITORIAL AREA
OBSERVATIONS: Wet mop nesting inside mop bucket. Hang mop to air dry. Correct today. REPEAT VIOLATION

CODE DESCRIPTION: At least one janitorial sink equipped with hot and cold water and a drain shall be provided and conveniently located for the cleaning of mops or similar wet floor cleaning tools and for the disposal of mop water and similar liquid waste. A room, area, or cabinet separated from food preparation, food storage, or warewashing shall be provided for the storage of cleaning equipment and supplies. After use, mops shall be placed in a position that allows them to air-dry without soiling walls, equipment, or supplies. (114279, 114281, 114282)
# 45 FLOOR, WALL, AND CEILING FINISHES
OBSERVATIONS: - Found gap between ceiling panels above grill cold top cooler. Eliminate gap within 2 days.
- Found holes/gaps in the following locations: on wall behind bar soup warmer, above water heater, behind pizza oven, around plumbing above soda syrup boxes, on wall next to hanging equipment (inside water heater room). Seal all holes/gaps with approved material within 30 days.
- Chipped floor tiles and some tiles are bulging out in front of pizza oven. Repair within 4 months.
CODE DESCRIPTION: Food facilities shall be fully enclosed in a building consisting of permanent floors, walls, and an overhead structure that meets minimum standards. The walls and ceiling shall be smooth, durable, nonabsorbent, and easily cleanable. Flooring and base coving shall be smooth, durable, and made of approved nonabsorbent material that is easily cleanable. Floor surfaces shall be coved at the juncture of the floor and wall with a 3/8 inch minimum radius and shall extend up the wall at least 4 inches. (114143 (d), 114266, 114268, 114268.1, 114271, 114272)
