# 7 HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES
OBSERVATIONS: Top layer of meatballs was measured between 124F-132F. Maintain hot food items at/above 135F at all times. Corrected by reheating above 165F.

Minor Violations: Overfilled container with sliced roast beef 45F-47F, sliced cheeses 45F, and teriyaki chicken 45F stored at cold top cooler. Do not overfill insert fill containers; store above 3/4 insert full. Corrected during inspection.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be

held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return

to the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

# 1c FOOD HANDLER CARDS

OBSERVATIONS: Several employees have expired food handler card. Renew within 30 days.

MAJOR

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food handlers that prepare, handle, or serve non-prepackaged potentially hazardous food, shall obtain a valid Food Handler Card within 30 days after date of hire. (113948)
# 6 HANDWASHING FACILITIES
OBSERVATIONS: Empty soap dispenser was found in the back handwash station (also used as a prep sink). Another hand washing station fully stocked available nearby and there was no food prep or warewashing conducted at time of arrival. Employee placed soap during inspection. REPEAT VIOLATION
CODE DESCRIPTION: Handwashing facilities shall be provided within or adjacent to toilet rooms, food preparation, and warewashing areas. Handwashing facilities shall be clean, unobstructed, and accessible at all times. Handwashing facilities shall be provided with soap and sanitary single-use towels or heated-air hand drying device in approved dispensers. Dispensers shall be maintained in good repair. (113953, 113953.1, 113953.2)
# 14a FOOD CONTACT SURFACES CLEANED AND SANITIZED
OBSERVATIONS: - Residue build up on tea and soda nozzles. Clean properly and maintain. Correct today.
- Found one piece of human hair on top of cutting board at prep line. Employee removed cutting board and placed inside 2-comp warewash sink for warewashing. Ensure all food contact surfaces are clean and sanitized at all times. Keep employee hair restrained.
CODE DESCRIPTION: Food contact surfaces, utensils, and equipment shall be cleaned and sanitized at the following times: before each use with different type of raw food of animal origin, when changing from raw food to ready to eat food, between uses with raw produce and potentially hazardous food, before using a thermometer, any time during the operation when contamination may have occurred. Food-contact surfaces and utensils shall be clean to sight and touch. (114113, 114115, 114117, 114125(b), 114141)
# 27 FOOD PROTECTED FROM CONTAMINATION
OBSERVATIONS: Coffee cups stored next to front handwash sink faucet. Store food away from handwash station to prevent from contamination. Corrected.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Food shall be protected from contamination during transportation, storage, preparation, holding, and display. # 32b COMPLIANCE WITH TRANS FAT BAN
OBSERVATIONS: Facility is missing nutritional panel and/or ingredients for brownies. Panels must be available to verify that all non-prepackaged food items are trans-fat free. All panels must be available by the next routine inspection. Foods that have 0.5 grams of trans fat per serving or higher shall not be served from facility.
CODE DESCRIPTION: No food containing artificial trans fat may be stored, distributed, served by, or used in the preparation of any food within a food facility. Every food facility shall maintain on the premises, the label for any food that contains fat, oil, or shortening, for as long as this food is stored, distributed, served by, or used in the preparation of food within the food facility. Food sold or served in a manufacturer's original sealed package is exempt from this requirement. (114377)
# 33 NONFOOD-CONTACT SURFACES CLEAN
OBSERVATIONS: Brown residue build up inside back handwash sink / prep sink. Per employee, they wash vegetables like tomatoes and cucumbers in a bowl at this sink and then drain it. Clean sink today. Provide colander for washing vegetables within 3 days. Ensure sink is washed and sanitized before washing vegetables.
CODE DESCRIPTION: Nonfood-contact surfaces of equipment shall be kept free of an accumulation of dust, dirt, food residue, and other debris. Interior premises of a food facility shall be kept clean. (114115 (b), 114257)
# 35 EQUIPMENT APPROVED AND MAINTAINED
OBSERVATIONS: Observed cardboard boxes reused in this food facility for equipment storage (like box of pepperoni reused for cup/lid storage). Cease practice immediately. Remove reused cardboard boxes. All surfaces must be smooth and easily washable.
CODE DESCRIPTION: Utensils and equipment shall be kept fully operative and in good repair. All new and replacement equipment shall be properly installed and be certified by NSF, UL Sanitation, or ETL Sanitation. Facilities that wash, rinse, soak, thaw, or similarly prepare foods shall be provided with a approved food preparation sink. Surfaces such as cutting blocks and boards that are subject to scratching and scoring shall be resurfaced or replaced if they can no longer be effectively cleaned and sanitized. (114130, 114130.1, 114130.2, 114130.3, 114130.4, 114130.5, 114130.6, 114132, 114133, 114137, 114139, 114153, 114163, 114165, 114169, 114175, 114177, 114180)
# 36 EQUIPMENT STORED IN APPROVED LOCATION
OBSERVATIONS: Tomato cutter hanging on rim of back handwash /prep sink. Also, ladle was stored next to front handwash sink. All equipment must be stored in hygienic manner and away from possible contamination. Corrected.
CODE DESCRIPTION: Clean equipment, utensils, linens, and single-use articles shall be stored at least six inches above the floor in an approved location within the fully enclosed permitted food facility. Clean equipment, utensils, linens, and single-use articles shall not be exposed to splash, dust, vermin or other forms of contamination. Non-food items shall be stored and displayed separate from food and food-contact surfaces. During pauses in food preparation utensils shall be stored with handles above the top of the food, on a clean sanitized surface, in running water, or in water that is at least 135F. Single-use articles and multiservice utensils shall be handled, displayed, and dispensed so that contamination of food and lip-contact surfaces is prevented. Pressurized cylinders shall be securely fastened to rigid structure. (114074, 114081, 114119, 114121, 114161, 114172, 114178, 114179, 114083, 114185, 114185.2, 114185.3, 114185.4, 114185.5)

# 41 PLUMBING FIXTURES AND BACKFLOW PREVENTION
OBSERVATIONS: Found black insert between floor sink and drain pipe beneath back prep table. Remove obstruction to ensure 1 inch air gap is provided. Correct within 3 days.
CODE DESCRIPTION: Steam tables, ice machines, ice bins, food preparation sinks, display cases, walk-in refrigeration units, and other similar equipment that discharge liquid waste shall drain into a floor sink with an air gap or other approved type of receptor. Plumbing and plumbing fixtures shall be maintained so as to prevent contamination, shall be kept clean, fully operative, and in good repair. The potable water supply shall be protected with a backflow or back siphonage protection device. Any hose used for potable water shall be of approved materials, labeled, properly stored, and used for no other purpose. (114171, 114190, 114192, 114193, 114193.1, 114199, 114201, 114269)
# 44c MOP SINK AND JANITORIAL AREA
OBSERVATIONS: Wet mop nesting on the floor. Provide hanger and hang wet mop to air dry.

CODE DESCRIPTION: At least one janitorial sink equipped with hot and cold water and a drain shall be provided and conveniently located for the cleaning of mops or similar wet floor cleaning tools and for the disposal of mop water and similar liquid waste. A room, area, or cabinet separated from food preparation, food storage, or warewashing shall be provided for the storage of cleaning equipment and supplies. After use, mops shall be placed in a position that allows them to air-dry without soiling walls, equipment, or supplies. (114279, 114281, 114282)
# 45 FLOOR, WALL, AND CEILING FINISHES
OBSERVATIONS: - Gaps on ceiling near electrical panels boxes in the back area. REPEAT VIOLATION - Gaps on ceiling above warewash sink. - Gaps observed around plumbing inside cabinet beneath front handwash sink. - Broken cove base tiles near the mop sink. - Chipped flooring observed near reach-in cooler storing toaster oven. Repair and seal holes with approved material within 30 days.
CODE DESCRIPTION: Food facilities shall be fully enclosed in a building consisting of permanent floors, walls, and an overhead structure that meets minimum standards. The walls and ceiling shall be smooth, durable, nonabsorbent, and easily cleanable. Flooring and base coving shall be smooth, durable, and made of approved nonabsorbent material that is easily cleanable. Floor surfaces shall be coved at the juncture of the floor and wall with a 3/8 inch minimum radius and shall extend up the wall at least 4 inches. (114143 (d), 114266, 114268, 114268.1, 114271, 114272)
