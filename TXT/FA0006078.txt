# 7 HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES
OBSERVATIONS: The following potentially hazardous food items were stored at improper holding temperature (all stored in walk in cooler #1):
- one tray of egg and flour mixture measured at 49F-50F - one tray of bbq pork measured at 52F (cooked 4 hours prior to inspection) - one tray of chicken with noodle measured at 48F-49F - one plate of chicken with vegetable measured at 50F - three bags of oysters measured at 45F
Corrected; ALL food items noted above were relocated to the other walk in cooler with ambient temperature of 41F. Service unit within 24 hours and do not store any potentially hazardous food items in this walk in cooler until the unit can hold ambient temperature of 41F and below.
CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return to the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)
# 23 VERMIN AND ANIMAL CONTAMINATION
OBSERVATIONS: Observed 18 dead cockroaches in the following locations:
- 1 on glue trap on floor below motors for fish tanks - 3 on floor below motors for fish tanks - 6 on floor below prep table where dough mixer is located - 4 on floor below steam table in customer area
The most recent pest control report, dated 10/27/15, was available at time of inspection. Continue with pest control, clean, sanitize and maintain the areas today.
CODE DESCRIPTION: A food facility shall at all times be equipped, maintained, and operated as to prevent the entrance and harborage of animals, birds, and vermin, including, but not limited to rodents and insects. (114259, 114259.1, 114259.4, 114259.5)
# 26 THAWING METHODS
OBSERVATIONS: Facility uses two stand alone fans when thawing frozen meat. Discontinue this practice. Ensure a correct method of thawing is used. Provided thawing sticker.
CODE DESCRIPTION: Frozen foods shall be stored or displayed in their frozen state unless being thawed in the following manner: under refrigeration, completely submerged under cold running water with sufficient velocity to flush loose particles, in microwave oven, during the cooking process. (114018, 114020)

# 40 WIPING CLOTHS STORAGE AND USE
OBSERVATIONS: Measured one sanitizer bucket located in steam table area at 0 ppm chlorine. Maintain at least 100 ppm chlorine. Corrected during inspection.

CODE DESCRIPTION: Wiping cloths used to clean food and nonfood-contact surfaces shall be used only once unless kept in clean water with a sanitizer solution of 100 ppm available chlorine or 200 ppm quaternary ammonium. Sponges shall not be used on food-contact surfaces. Except for fabric implements, linen shall not be used in contact with food. (114099.6, 114135, 114185.1, 114185.3 (d-e)).
