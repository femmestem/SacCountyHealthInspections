# 7 HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES
OBSERVATIONS: White sauce (chicken stock based) at 85 F on cooks line. Per employee, this sauce is made from hot chicken stock when it is ordered and kept 1-2 hours. No time log in place for this item. This batch was discarded and will be remade per order. If it is held outside temperature control, a time log must be used.
Cooked noodles at 56 F on shelf. This item is only used on a time log during lunch. No time log used at this time of day. Noodles had been taken out of refrigeration for an order approximately 30 minutes before and were replaced in refrigeration.
CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return to the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

MAJOR

# 9 COOLING METHODS
OBSERVATIONS: Bowls of meat cooling on top of prep line inserts. Meat had been cooked within 1 hour and was above 70 F. Do not cool on top of prep line, cool in a refrigeration unit capable of reducing temperature to 41 F or below. Corrected.
CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be rapidly cooled from 135F to 70F within 2 hours, and then from 70F to 41F within 4 hours. Cooling shall be by one or more of the following methods: in shallow containers, separating food into smaller portions, adding ice as an ingredient, using an ice bath, stirring frequently, using rapid cooling equipment, or using containers that facilitate heat transfer. (114002, 114002.1) # 33 NONFOOD-CONTACT SURFACES CLEAN
OBSERVATIONS: Food debris in bottom of upright reach in freezer by dishwasher.
Build up on doors and gaskets of prep refrigerator. Clean - 1 day. 4TH REPEAT VIOLATION
CODE DESCRIPTION: Nonfood-contact surfaces of equipment shall be kept free of an accumulation of dust, dirt, food residue, and other debris. Interior premises of a food facility shall be kept clean. (114115 (b), 114257)
