# 7 HOT AND COLD HOLDING TEMPERATURES
OBSERVATIONS: Taco meat at 92F, steak at 93F, chicken at 90F at steam table. Maintain at 135F or above. Discarded voluntarily during inspection.
One hot holding unit/ Steam table not working. Don't use until repaired.
CODE DESCRIPTION: Potentially hazardous foods shall be maintained at or below 41F or at or above 135F. The following foods may be held at or below 45F: raw shell eggs, unshucked live molluscan shellfish, pasteurized milk and pasteurized milk products in original sealed containers, potentially hazardous foods held during transportation. Food preparation shall not exceed two cumulative hours without a return to the required holding temperatures. (113996, 113998, 114037)

MAJOR

# 1a DEMONSTRATION OF KNOWLEDGE
OBSERVATIONS: Employee did not know how to track time for sliced tomatoes to use time as health control. Explained during inspection. Train all employees today.
CODE DESCRIPTION: Food employees shall have adequate knowledge of food safety as it relates to their assigned duties. (113947)
# 8 TIME AS A PUBLIC HEALTH CONTROL
OBSERVATIONS: Employee tracking time incorrectly. Facility uses time as health control for tomatoes and use them within 4 hours; tomatoes were brought out of refrigerator at 1:45pm but the time was rounded up to to start at 2 PM and to end at 6 pm. Correct immediately. Discard time must not be more than 4 hours from the time the food items are removed from temperature control. Corrected during inspection. Train all employees.
CODE DESCRIPTION: When time as a public health control is used, the following procedures shall be observed: food items shall be marked to indicate four hours past the time when the food was removed from temperature control, food shall be served or discarded within the four-hour time limit, written procedures for food that was prepared, cooked, and refrigerated before time as a public health control was used, shall be maintained at the food facility and be made available to the enforcement agency upon request. (114000) # 33 NONFOOD-CONTACT SURFACES CLEAN
OBSERVATIONS: -Cabinet under the soda machine in dining area not clean. Clean within 3 days.
-Floor under the steam table/green burrito area not clean. Clean within 3 days.
-Surface around the heating lamp over the fried food not clean (grease build up). Clean within 3 days.
CODE DESCRIPTION: Nonfood-contact surfaces of equipment shall be kept free of an accumulation of dust, dirt, food residue, and other debris. Interior premises of a food facility shall be kept clean. (114115 (b), 114257) # 41 PLUMBING FIXTURES AND BACKFLOW PREVENTION
OBSERVATIONS: Drainage pipe from refrigeator to floor sink lacking air gap. Provide at least 1 inch air gap between the pipe opening and flood rim level of floor sink within 5 days.

# 45 FLOOR, WALL, AND CEILING FINISHES
OBSERVATIONS: The grout eroded in the floor tiles in dishwashing area and near green burrito station. One base coving tiles broken in freezer.

Repair within 3 weeks.
CODE DESCRIPTION: Food facilities shall be fully enclosed in a building consisting of permanent floors, walls, and an overhead structure that meets minimum standards. The walls and ceiling shall be smooth, durable, nonabsorbent, and easily cleanable. Flooring and base coving shall be smooth, durable, and made of approved nonabsorbent material that is easily cleanable. Floor surfaces shall be coved at the juncture of the floor and wall with a 3/8 inch minimum radius and shall extend up the wall at least 4 inches.
